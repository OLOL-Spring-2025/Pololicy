PURPOSE 
The laboratory manages correction of laboratory records in a way that clearly conveys the 
intended information without obliterating the original documentation.  This applies to all 
Laboratory records including Quality Control data, Temperature Logs, and intermediate test 
results or worksheets. 
 
POLICY     
Laboratory records and changes to such records will be legible and indelible.  
Original (erroneous) entries must remain visible.  Erasures, white and correction fluid are 
unacceptable. Electronic records and corrections to the record are accessible. Corrected data, 
including the identity of the person changing the record and when the record was changed, will 
be accessible.  
 
PROCEDURE 
1. Upon making or detecting a written error, the original recording will be crossed out using 
a single line.  
2. The person responsible for the change will initial and date the change.  
3. Information will not be scratched out beyond a single line.  
4. Information will not be overwritten.   
5. Pencil will not be used.  Records and changes to records must never be erased. 
6. White out will not be used. 
  
SUPERVISORY REVIEW 
1. Upon performing supervisory review of written records (QC, Downtime/offline results, 
Worksheets, etc.), reviewer will make note of any written errors not corrected following 
the appropriate procedure. 
a. Asterisk the incorrectly documented error 
b. At the bottom of the page, add another asterisk and acknowledge the incorrectly 
documented error. 
c. Address with staff that incorrectly documented the error.
