PURPOSE 
Discharge planning is facilitated by the multidisciplinary team in partnership with the patient. Discharge 
planning is intended to provide a smooth transition from acute care to post-acute care (PAC) with a 
focus on the patient’s goals of care and treatment preference. 
 
POLICY 
1. Discharge planning begins on admission and continues throughout the patient’s hospitalization. 
2. Care Management and/or Nursing team members will coordinate discharge planning in collaboration 
with the patient’s attending physician.   
3. Care Management and/or Nursing team members will assist in planning for a patient’s post-acute 
care in collaboration with the patient and their family member or support person. 
4. Care Management will provide community resource information to patients and families to aid the 
patient and/or family member/support person in the decision-making process. 
5. Care Management will complete an assessment of the patient prior to discussing discharge planning 
options with the multidisciplinary team, patient, and/or patient’s family members/support person.   
a. The assessment may include but is not limited to:  
i. Need for medical services  
ii. Home environment 
iii. Insurance or financial barriers. 
iv. Support system. 
v. Community resources 
vi. Transportation  
vii. Social Determinates of Health. 
6. Discharge planning will be documented in the patient’s medical record.
