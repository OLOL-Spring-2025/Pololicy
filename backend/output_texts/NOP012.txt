Title: Food/Drug Interactions 
Policy 
Reference #: NOP-012 
Supersedes #: 8/99 
Date of Origination: November, 1993 Review Date:  June 10, 2024 
Revision Date: March 25, 2022 
     
PURPOSE: 
To provide education to patients, families, and significant others regarding medications, diet, and 
food/drug interactions. 
 
POLICY: 
1. A Medical Staff approved list of medications for educational purposes will be presented in an 
educational format to patients, families and caregivers who have those medications ordered. Special 
attention will be directed toward the food/drug interactions. 
2. A copy of the Food & Drug Interactions and the Medications & Diet pamphlet will be provided in 
the admission packet for every patient admitted to OLOL.  
 
PROCEDURES: 
 
Education 
1. Nursing, pharmacy and Clinical Nutrition personnel may provide Food/Drug interaction education. 
2. Prior to patients discharge, the authorized healthcare provider will instruct the patient, patient's 
family and/or caregiver about the medication, potential food interactions and diet restrictions.  
3. The authorized healthcare provider instructing the patient will document the education in the 
Interdisciplinary Patient Education Record form. 
 
Intervention 
1. Nutritional Services receives from the hospital computer system, a list of patients receiving 
medications requiring dietary modifications and/or restrictions. 
2. This list will be reviewed by a Registered Dietitian, or the designee, Dietetic Technician who shall 
be responsible for modifying the patient's diet.
