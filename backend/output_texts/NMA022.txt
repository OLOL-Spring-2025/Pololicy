PURPOSE: 
Medication reconciliation is an interdisciplinary process to ensure a review of the patient’s home 
medications and the physician’s orders upon admission/intake, transfer from intensive care, post-surgical, 
and discharge. 
 
POLICY: 
Home medications will be documented by nursing as soon as possible upon arrival to nursing unit  
 
For the purpose of reconciliation, the term “home medications” includes: 
▪ Prescription Medications ▪ Over the counter medications 
▪ Sample medications ▪ Investigational/study medications 
▪ Herbal remedies ▪ Vitamins & other supplements 
▪ Parenteral nutrition ▪ Dietary/Nutritional supplements 
▪ Blood derivatives ▪ Eye, ear, skin preparations/patches 
▪ IV solutions, plain or with   
electrolytes or medications 
▪ Inhaled medications and respiratory   
treatments 
 
PROCEDURE: 
1. Upon admission/intake, licensed personnel will obtain a list of home medications from the patient 
and/or family members.  The list should include dosage, frequency, routes and date and time of last 
dose when available. 
2. The physician is responsible for reviewing the home medication list and continuing or discontinuing 
medication as appropriate using the following “Reconciliation” forms in the Electronic Medical 
Record (EMR): 
a. “Admission” – As soon as possible after admission/intake 
b. “Transfer” – Prior to transfer out of PACU or Critical Care Unit 
c. “Discharge” – Prior to patient discharge, as part of the Depart Process
