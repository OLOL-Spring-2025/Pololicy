PURPOSE: 
Provide for the collection laboratory patient testing that has been ordered but not collected (utilizing EPIC 
Hyperspace: Patient Draw List Application) 
 
Addresses: 
Venipuncture Charge Techs 
Venipuncture Phlebotomists 
 
POLICY: 
A “Patient Draw List” report will be printed several times during the course of each shift by the Charge Tech or 
designated person.  The report contains requests for laboratory service that have been ordered but not collected.  
The report excludes test requests ordered as “Unit Collects” and other non-blood laboratory orders.  Venipuncture 
staff researches test requests listed on the report to determining current order status.  Action is taken to either 
collect missed samples or to cancel unwanted orders.  Patient collections will disappear from the Patient Draw 
List as phlebotomist complete assigned draws. 
 
A. Outstanding Orders 
1. Determine the reason for the outstanding patient order 
- Dialysis to draw 
- Patient unavailable for drawing 
- Patient refusing 
- Patient receiving blood 
- Missed draw 
2. Reassign outstanding orders 
3. Call the patient’s unit to determine current availability 
4. Assign a Phlebotomist to collect the tests 
5. If the patient continues to be unavailable, document the time of contact and name of unit person 
spoken to in the Rover Device and on the Phlebotomist draw sheet 
6. Charge Tech will periodically check with unit personnel for patient availability and either have the 
patient drawn or the test canceled 
 
B. Printing a “Patient Draw Report 
1. Log in to EPIC Hyperspace application 
2. Open the “Patient Lists” icon 
3. New orders are released at 00:31, 05:05, 07:05, 08:05, 12:05, 16:05, and 20:05 daily 
4. Assign all collections to phlebotomy staff
