PURPOSE: 
To determine responsibility for monitoring negative pressure for patient rooms. 
 
POLICY:  
1. Nursing monitors daily the maintenance of negative airflow pressure in isolation rooms when 
Airborne Isolation is ordered. 
 
2. Plant services assigns qualified vendor to test and inspect each room annually.  Monitoring is done 
daily through scheduled rounding. 
 
PROCEDURES:  
  
1. Nursing will monitor  that negative air has been maintained when the patient is on Airborne 
Precautions by one of the following methods 
a. Electronic  monitor has a red and a green light. Check to ensure that the green light is on, 
indicating that the room has negative pressure. 
b. Ball-in-the-wall is used with the red ball for negative pressure rooms.  When walking 
through hall, the red ball should not be visualized. 
 
2. The door of the room must remain closed to maintain the negative airflow in all areas of the hospital.  
If the isolation room has two doors both doors must be closed to have negative airflow.  
 
3. Contact Plant Services: 
• if the electronic monitor has no light or if the red light is on  
• if the red ball is visualized while outside the room in the hall
