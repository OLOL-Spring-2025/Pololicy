PURPOSE: 
Provide for acceptable disposal of needles and other kinds of sharps 
 
Addresses: 
Venipuncture Charge Techs 
Venipuncture Phlebotomists 
 
POLICY: 
Needles and other sharps are to be disposed of in a manner that minimizes risk of accidental exposure to 
healthcare workers and complies with state infectious waste management regulations. 
 
PROCEDURE: 
A. Sharps Disposal Containers should be placed in areas that are accessible to personnel involved in the 
delivery of care to the patient. 
1. Each patient room contains a Sharps Disposal Container 
2. Each phlebotomy tray should be equipped with a Sharps Disposal Container  
3. Each blood drawing station should have a Sharps Disposal Container placed in convenient proximity 
of the drawing phlebotomist 
B. Needles should be discarded intact, uncut, and uncapped into a Sharps Container immediately following use 
C. Non Sharp materials should not be discarded in sharps containers 
D. Keep the sharp box lid closed, open lid to dispose of a sharp product and then close 
E. Fill sharps containers to the manufacturers recommended full line marked on the side of the container 
F. Sharps box lids are to be securely locked closed prior to disposal 
G. Place closed sharps containers in the storage are in phlebotomy for pick up by the waste contractor 
H. Sharps are considered a potentially infectious waste and are manifested to a contracted waste disposal 
handler for incineration
