PURPOSE: 
 
The program is for the administration of the Tetanus, Diphtheria, acellular Pertussis (Tdap)  vaccine to 
employees who have contact with patients less than 12 months of age .  The vaccination is offered once 
during the course of a team member’s employment  on a voluntary basis after the employee has read the 
Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) .  
Vaccination is only administered after obtaining informed written consent.  All documentation of this 
vaccination program is maintained by Employee Health. 
 
POLICY: 
 
Employees who have contact with patients less than 12 months of age will be offered the Tdap vaccine at 
no charge.  The  Tdap vaccine will be offered after review of the employee’s immunization records, if 
available, and during the first week of Orientation  or any time thereafter.  Current employees who have 
contact with patients less than 12 months of age who have not previously received a dose of Tdap will also 
be offered the vaccination.  Employees, who originally decline to take the vaccine but later decide that 
they want the vaccine, may come to Employee Health for the vaccine at any time. 
 
PROCEDURE: 
 
I. Education: 
a. Dispersal of educational information, which includes the VIS and consent form. 
 
II. Prior Vaccination: 
a. Employees will be asked to bring in documentation of their most recent tetanus vaccination 
or documentation of a Tdap vaccination. 
b. Employees with documentation of a prior Tdap will not receive another dose.   The 
employee will be instructed to follow -up with their physician for a Td booster every 10 
years or sooner if there is an injury. 
 
III. Immunization: 
a. Employees between the ages of 19 and 64 who have contact with patients under the age of 
12 months will be offered one dose of Tdap.   
b. One dose of Tdap, 0.5 ml, will be administered intramuscularly in the deltoid muscle.  2 
c. Precautions (as listed in informed consent , VIS,  and package insert) will be addressed.  
Employees who fall into these precautionary areas will need written permission from their 
attending physician to receive the vaccine. 
d. The vaccine is administered by Employee Health staff or their designee  at no cost to the 
employee. 
 
IV. Documentation: 
a. Employee Health will maintain completed documentation records , which includes signed 
consent form, lot numbers, site of injection, person administering dose, and date. 
 
V. Adverse Reaction Reporting: 
a. The VAERS (Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System)  form will be used to report 
adverse reactions.  The form is available online at www.vaers.hhs.gov.
