PURPOSE: 
To provide guidelines for central line dressing change and site care.  
 
 
POLICY:  
The hospital adheres to the Evidence-Based practices of the IHI’s Central Line Bundle and CDC’s 
Prevention of Intravascular Infections recommendations. 
 
A. May be performed only by a Registered Nurse 
B. Applies to central line catheters, midline catheters and implanted devices which have been accessed 
C. Transparent dressings with Chlorhexidine Gluconate (CHG) are changed every 7 days 
D. If gauze is used to control oozing, gauze and overlying transparent dressings are changed every 48 
hours or more frequently if ordered by physician.   
E. Change dressing immediately if loose, wet or soiled, or if skin integrity is compromised. 
F. PICC or midline catheter’s securement device is changed with each dressing change and covered 
with transparent dressing 
G. Needleless connector caps are changed with infusion line changes or every 96 hours if saline/heparin 
locked. 
H. Antimicrobial caps are to be utilized on all needless connectors not in use, if a cap is not in use, 
needleless connectors should be scrubbed with an alcohol prep pad for 15-30 seconds (with a 
minimum of 15 seconds) and allowed to air dry prior to each access. 
  
I. Use aseptic technique and sterile supplies.
