PURPOSE: 
To define neutropenia, provide formula for calculating absolute neutrophil count and to describe the 
procedure necessary to institute neutropenic precautions. 
 
POLICY: 
All neutropenic patients will be cared for following established standards. 
 
PROCEDURES: 
An Absolute Neutrophil Count (ANC) of less than 1000 constitutes a level of neutropenia that requires 
extra precautions: 
 
1. ANC = (Segs + Bands) x WBC 
2. Neutropenic precaution sign is placed on the patientâ€™s door. 
3. The patient is not to receive fresh flowers or plants. 
4. A regular diet is ordered as long as the fresh fruits and vegetable have been washed. Physician 
discretion will be used on prolonged neutropenia patients such as acute myeloid leukemia and 
post-transplant patients.  
5. Temperatures are to be taken every 4 hours. 
6. Meticulous handwashing by all who enter the room is strictly enforced. 
7. No staff member with a transmissible disease or recent immunization will have contact with 
patient. 
8. Invasive procedures are kept at a minimum. 
9. The patient will be encouraged to perform meticulous personal hygiene. 
10. Institute mouth care protocol. 
11. No visitors with transmissible infections will be allowed to visit the patient.
