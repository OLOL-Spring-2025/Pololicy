Purpose: 
 
To establish guidelines for the termination of Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation exercise or activity. 
 
 
Policy: 
 
Exercise or activity will be discontinued if any of the following responses occur: 
 
• Patient requests to stop. 
• Failure of monitoring equipment. 
 • Light-headedness, confusion, ataxia, pallor, cyanosis, dyspnea, nausea, or any peripheral circulatory      
 insufficiency. 
 • Progressive angina with exercise. 
 • Symptomatic supraventricular tachycardia. 
 • ST displacement (greater than 3 mm horizontal or down sloping from rest). 
 • Ventricular tachycardia (3 or more consecutive PVCs). 
 • Exercise induced left or right bundle branch block. 
 • Onset of second and/or third degree heart block. 
 • R on T PVCs (one). 
 • Frequent unifocal PVCs (>30% of complexes). 
 • Frequent multifocal PVCs (>30% of complexes). 
 • Couplets (greater than 2 per minute). 
 • Increase in heart rate greater than recommended in exercise prescription. 
 • Drop of 10 mm Hg or more in systolic blood pressure. 
 • Excessive blood pressure rise (systolic >220 mm Hg, diastolic >110 mm Hg). 
 • Inappropriate bradycardia (drop in heart rate >10 bpm) with increase or no change in work load. 
• Oxygen Saturation falls below 85% and cannot be increased to 90% with supplemental oxygen up to 
 5 liters nasal cannula. 
• Inappropriate shortness of breath, fatigue, or other atypical symptoms for the patient which could 
 compromise the patient’s safety in the clinical estimation of the Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation staff. 
• The patient is unable/unwilling to follow the exercise prescription. 
 
 
Procedure: 
 
If one or more of the above mentioned symptoms occur, the patient will stop exercise. A staff member 
shall contact the physician for further instructions. 
 
An ECG strip as well as written documentation shall be recorded in the patient's permanent chart. 
Summary information will also be documented on the Untoward Event Documentation form and 
patient's exercise record.
