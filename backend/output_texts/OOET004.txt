SCOPE: 
Applies to Our Lady of the Lake Hospital, Inc. (“OLOL”) and its wholly owned sponsored organizations (collectively 
referred to herein as “Entity”) that propose and adopt Facility wide policies. 
PURPOSE: 
Our Lady of the Lake Regional Medical Center (OLOL) provides guidance to employees in carrying out their 
health care roles in a manner consistent with the values and ethics embodied in the philosophy and mission of 
the Franciscan Missionaries of Our Lady Health System (FMOLHS). 
 
In seeking guidance in matters of ethics and professional conduct, Team Members may contact their 
supervisors, FMOLHS Bioethicist, and the Lake’s Compliance Officer.  OLOL receives guidance from the 
FMOLHS, the Catholic Health Association, The Ethical and Religious Directives for Catholic Health Care 
Services, the Ordinary of the Diocese of Baton Rouge, the OLOL Ethics Committee as well as the given 
employee’s professional organization(s). 
 
Employees must maintain professional standards and promote OLOL’s commitment to human dignity and the 
common good (Ethical and Religious Directives for Catholic Health Care Services, Sixth Edition, #9). 
 
Note:  The information described herein does not create any legal rights and OLOL reserves the right to 
deviate from, revise or eliminate this policy, at any time, in its sole discretion. 
 
PROCEDURE: 
1. Personnel shall be responsible for knowing and aspiring to the written directives of OLOL, as well as the 
professional standards and Code of Ethics of their profession or position.  Additional guidance may be 
found in regulations and standards outlined by federal, state, and other regulatory or accrediting agencies. 
 
2. Personnel shall use ethical practices in the course of their work, which include matters of respect for the 
dignity of life, confidentiality, and conflict of interest. Personnel shall not discuss or divulge information 
which they learn in the course of their duties that is not related directly to patient care.
