POLICY: 
 
The pharmacy shall have current drug information available for professional staff. 
 
SCOPE OF DRUG INFORMATION SERVICE 
 
The scope of the drug information service shall include information on new products and existing 
products. 
 
DRUG INFORMATION SOURCES 
 
The pharmacy's primary drug information source is its required compendia of reference material. 
 
Secondary drug information sources include other healthcare organizations, academic institutions, and 
organized drug information services/centers. 
 
DRUG INFORMATION FORMATS 
 
Information formats may include verbal communications, in -service education, newsletters, and drug 
information notices. 
 
PHARMACY REFERENCE COMPENDIA 
 
The pharmacy shall maintain references required by the Board of Pharmacy, other agencies, or law.  These 
references shall address, but not be limited to: 
 
• Drug interactions 
• Drug therapy 
• Side effects 
• Toxicology 
• Dosage 
• Indications for use 
• Routes of administration 
• Current federal and state drug law 
information

The pharmacy shall ensure that references material is readily available in all patient care areas. 
 
The following references or information shall be available in areas determined by the Director of 
Pharmacy: 
 
• Metric -apothecaries weight and measure conversion charts. 
• Poison control center telephone number. 
 
REFERENCES TO BE READILY AVAILABLE 
 
The following references or information shall be readily available to the professionals who require them: 
 
Formulary. 
• Information on emergency pharmacy services and/or how to contact a pharmacist. 
• Telephone numbers of emergency organizations (e.g., poison control centers). 
• Poison antidote charts or information. 
• Drug interaction and contraindication information. 
• Written guidelines for preparing and labeling sterile products. 
• Other materials and information at the discretion of the Director of Pharmacy.
