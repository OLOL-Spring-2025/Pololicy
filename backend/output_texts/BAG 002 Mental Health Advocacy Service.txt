POLICY:        
Legal counsel and representation is available for patients to ensure their legal rights are protected. 
 
PROCEDURE: 
1. Information about the Mental Health Advocacy Service is available to each MBH patient. 
2. The phone number may be provided in the patient handbook and posted is on each MBH unit. 
      3. If a patient is unable to read or understand the information, the staff will explain the service to the patient. 
      4. Patients have access to a telephone 24 hours daily for use in phoning the Mental Health Advocacy 
Service. 
      5. At the patient’s request, the Mental Health Advocate attorney, or the patient’s private attorney shall be 
given the opportunity to consult with his client whenever necessary to the performance of his duties.  
Adequate space and privacy shall be provided for the purpose of the attorney-client consultation. 
 a. Patient precautions should be explained to the patient’s attorney or Mental Health Advocacy by 
the patient’s nurse or Social Worker.  The attorney should understand that he/she is assuming 
responsibility for the patient’s safety while meeting with the patient.  This includes Q15, Q10, and 1:1 
observation.  The attorney can request that staff remain present.
