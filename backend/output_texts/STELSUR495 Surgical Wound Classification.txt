SUBJECT: 
                Surgical Wound Classification 
 
 
Page 1 of 2 pages 
  
 
 
 
Purpose:  
 To provide a standard classification system for evaluating surgical wound infections in 
relation to sources of contamination and degree of risk of infection.   
 
Applies To: 
Surgical Services, Infection Prevention 
Policy:   
 Surgical Wound Classification shall be documented on all surgical cases. 
Responsibility: 
 Surgical Services Personnel, Infection Control Nurse 
Procedure: 
RN circulator shall document Surgical Wound Classification on all surgeries using the 
following guidelines: 
 
1. CLASS I – CLEAN WOUNDS 
a. Nontraumatic 
b. No inflammation encountered 
c. No break in technique during surgery 
d. No contaminated spaces (gastrointestinal, respiratory, genitourinary, and genital) 
were encountered, and the wound was closed and drained, if necessary, with 
closed drains. 
 
2. CLASS II – CLEAN-CONTAMINATED WOUNDS 
a. Respiratory, urinary, gastrointestinal, or genital tracts were involved under 
controlled conditions and without unusual contamination. 
b. The genitourinary and biliary tracts may be entered in the absence of infection 
c. A minor break in an otherwise class I procedure would also fit into this class. 
 
 
3. CLASS III – CONTAMINATED WOUNDS 
a. Fresh traumatic or accidental wounds (less than 4 hours old) 
b. Major break in sterile surgical technique during procedure 
c. Gross contamination/spillage from gastrointestinal tract

d. Acute nonpurulent inflammation present at operative site 
e. Entry into the genitourinary or biliary tracts in presence of infected urine or bile 
 
 
4. CLASS IV – DIRTY AND INFECTED WOUNDS 
a. Old traumatic wounds (more than 4 hours old) or those with retained devitalized 
tissue, foreign body, fecal contamination, or delayed treatment or from a dirty 
source. 
b. A perforated viscera may be encountered 
c. Acute bacterial inflammation with pus encountered during the operation
