PURPOSE: 
To ensure the safety of patients, visitors, and team members in the event of a fire or to the conditions 
that indicate the presence of a fire. To prepare staff to appropriately respond to the danger fires present 
by addressing aspects of fire prevention, preparedness, control, and education.  To delineate authority, 
responsibilities, and procedures to follow in the event of a fire. 
 
POLICY: 
In the event of a fire, all staff will follow the Our Lady of the Lake Children’s Hospital (OLOLCH) 
written Code RED Safety and Response Plan to guide and prepare all in the proper response to fires.  
 
Contractors, students, licensed independent practitioners (LIP), and vendors are to follow the instruction 
of unit manager or designee. 
 
Each OLOLCH department will have their own Department Specific Code RED Safety and Response 
Plan. 
 
Each location’s Code RED Safety and Response Plan will be used in conjunction with the Emergency 
Operations Plan (ECEP001) and the OLOLCH Code RED Safety and Response Plan (CH-ECEP007) to 
guide the preparation and response in the event of a fire.

Specific roles of staff related to Code RED drills or Code RED response 
• Respiratory leadership or designee will clear the hallway of any carts or equipment by moving 
them to the RT storage area. 
• Respiratory leadership or designee will close doors to offices. 
• Respiratory staff will confirm automatic fire doors are closed 
• Respiratory staff will assist patients and visitors to safety explaining, “The hospital is conducting 
an emergency exercise to ensure your safety” 
• Respiratory staff will prevent people from using elevator. 
• Respiratory staff  will assist those with Limited English Proficiency (LEP) and those with 
sensory or mobility disabilities 
• Respiratory leadership or designee will ensure all employees are accounted for. 
• Respiratory leadership or designee will ensure all patients are accounted for. 
• Respiratory leadership or designee will ensure each room is empty. 
• Respiratory leadership or designee will confirm Area of Refuge occupants or those needing 
assistance have been evacuated. 
 
The location of medical gas shut off valves 
• The medical gas shut off valves are located in the hall outside the Respiratory Storage room and 
in the hall outside the Staff Lactation Room. 
 
Location and proper use of equipment for transporting patients between Fire/Smoke 
Compartments (horizontal and vertical evacuations) 
• If patient requires transport via transport ventilator, a transport ventilator with patient circuit will 
be available in the Respiratory equipment room adjacent to the Respiratory department.  All 
other supplies should be available on the nursing unit. 
 
Location and proper use of fire-fighting equipment and manual pull stations. 
• The closest fire extinguisher is in the hall outside the EVS closet. 
• The closest manual pull station is located outside stairwell 3 by the visitor elevators. 
 
Anything that makes the department unique from a life safety standpoint.  
• Oxygen cylinders may be located on a ventilator or Bipap device in the RT storage room. 
• Oxygen cylinders may be located in the ABG lab across the hall from the Respiratory 
department. 
• The Respiratory department and ABG lab has special badge access and the Code Red response 
team should have access. 
 
The location of the horizontal and vertical evacuation routes and destinations. 
• The primary horizontal evacuation route is through the double doors entering PEDS 4. 
• The primary vertical evacuation route is through PEDS 4 down Stairwell 7 gathering at the 
outside pavilion area. 
• The secondary horizontal evacuation route is through the double doors entering PICU. 
• The secondary vertical evacuation route is through PICU down Stairwell 1 gathering on the grass 
area near Constantin.
