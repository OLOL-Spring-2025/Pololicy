SUBJECT: 
                 
Criteria For Bariatric Surgery Patient Selection 
 
 
Page 1 of 2 
  
 
 
Purpose: 
 To provide a set of criteria required for all patients being considered for bariatric surgery 
to meet in order to qualify for surgery.  
   
 
Applies To: 
           Bariatric Surgeons 
Policy:   
 Bariatric surgery patient selection criteria shall be met by any patient having bariatric 
surgery. 
  
Procedure:  
 
Surgical intervention is proven treatment for patients with severe obesity. Patients may lose 
approximately 50-75 percent excess body weight over 12 to 24 months after surgery. As many as 20 
percent of patients will lose less than their desired amount of weight or will regain some weight.  
Carefully selected patients can achieve substantial and sustained weight reduction within one to two 
years postoperatively. The patient proposed for obesity surgery should be considered when the following 
criteria are met: 
 
Indications for Bariatric Surgery: 
 
ABSOLUTE: 
 
1. BMI > or = 40 or  
2. BMI > or = 35 with co-morbidities such as hypertension, severe OSA, diabetes mellitus, arthritis, 
dyslipidemia, or other serious cardiopulmonary condition. 
3. Patient is a maximum weight of 600 lbs and the patient ranging from 450 lbs-600 lbs will be 
considered on a case-by-case basis. 
4. The patient is at least 18 years of age, and patients over 70 will be considered on a case-by-case 
basis.

OTHER CONSIDERATIONS: 
 
1. The patient has no history of substance abuse or been documented to be in a controlled treatment 
program and is stabilized. 
2. The patient has made reasonable attempts at dieting and exercise for weight loss in a structured 
and documented program. 
3. The patient has suffered from obesity for a reasonable duration. 
4. Age, previous abdominal surgery, and previous bariatric surgery are NOT absolute 
contraindications. 
5. The patient has no absolute contraindications to major abdominal surgery. 
6. The patient has been fully counseled on the risks and benefits of the procedure. 
7. If patient under 18 years of age or over 70 years of age, case is reviewed by bariatric team 
8. Routine psychological testing/evaluation is necessary prior to surgery.  If a patient who is being 
considered for bariatric surgery has received an evaluation for behavioral health issues, then the 
mental health provider should indicate that the issue of surgery has been discussed with the 
patient, and that there are no identified contraindications to the proposed surgery from the 
viewpoint of the patient’s mental health. 
9. The patient has demonstrated an understanding of the need for post-surgical attention to lifestyle 
and dietary changes and is agreeable to long term follow-up. 
10. Medically unable to safely have an elective surgery procedure. 
 
 
NOTE: 
 
Patients with a BMI < 35 will be evaluated by the surgeon and internist to decide if bariatric surgery is 
recommended.  Patients with a BMI  75 and/or a weight > 600 lbs, will be evaluated by the bariatric 
team (Internist, Surgeon, MBS Coordinator, OR manager, Floor Manager, and Nutritionist). 
 
 
 
Contraindications for Bariatric Surgery: 
 
1. Active substance abuse 
2. Psychiatric disorder which is uncontrolled 
3. Defined noncompliance
