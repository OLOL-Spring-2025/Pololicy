Policy: 
  
Electrical power is supplied to the hospital by ENTERGY Corporation. 
Should a failure occur: 
  
Areas which are supplied by power from the EMERGENCY GENERATOR must include, but are 
not limited to: 
  
All Patient Care Areas  
Critical Equipment & Life Safety            
 
1. All Alarm Systems 
            2. Blood, Bone and Tissue Storage Units 
            3. Egress Illumination and Exit Signs  
            4. Elevators 
            5. Communication Systems (PBX and Paging System) 
            6. Medical Air and Medical/Surgical Vacuum Systems and Recovery Rooms 
            7. Special Care Units – ICU, Emergency Room 
            8. Steam Delivery System (at least one boiler) 
 9. Medication Refrigerators 
          10. Medication Dispensers 
  
Responsibility: 
  
Plant Operations 
  
Procedure: 
  
In the event of loss of electrical power the following procedures will be followed:  
 
1.      Emergency generators will start and supply emergency power to essential areas.  (see 
above list).

MANAGEMENT OF UTILITY SYSTEM FAILURE 
  
2.   The Plant Operations personnel will make sure that the generator is running properly   
       and then notify the Administrative House Supervisor/designee. Check all systems to  
      include the elevators, boilers, etc. 
  
3.    The Manager of Facilities  along with the Supervisor/designee will determine whether 
the loss of electrical power is due to external or internal disruption. 
  
4.      If the loss of electrical power is due to external disruption, the Plant Operations     
       Director or Supervisor/designee will contact ENTERGY, Acct #1997963 @ 1 -800-
766-1648 which provides electrical power at the 24/7 telephone number to determine 
the length of the downtime. Plant Operations supervisor/designee will inform the 
Administrative Supervisor/designee as cancellation of patient services may be 
required. 
  
5.     If the loss of electrical power is due to internal disruption, attempt to identify the  
problem. 
  
a.               Identify the distribution panel serving the effected area. 
b.              Trace and correct the problem from the distribution panel. 
c.               If the problem cannot be resolved immediately, notify the Administrative   
           House Supervisor/designee, and the affected areas. 
d.              If the problem is beyond the scope of the Plant Operations department, see   
           “Utility Emergency List” located in Plant Operations.  
e.                Emergency extension cords are located in the Plant Operations shop and can  
be used to receive power from  another area if there is critical need as 
determined by the department director. 
f.               When electrical power is restored, reset or restart equipment in the power  
plant, mechanical room, and other  parts of the hospital that have been affected 
by the power outage.
