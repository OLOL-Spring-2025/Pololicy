PURPOSE: Quality standard therapist/patient ratios are dependent upon patient acuity. 
POLICY: 
1. In general, the Rehab therapist’s caseload should consist of 6-8 patients. 
2. Support staff (e.g. – physical therapy aides) shall be used for both PT and OT. 
3. Aides shall in no way assist patients without a licensed therapist on premise giving continuous supervision. 
4. Each therapist may supervise no more than three aides at one time; more than two permittees; or more than 
five students. 
5. A licensed physical therapy assistant may treat a patient for up to 6 consecutive treatment days.  A licensed 
PT must reassess the patient and care plan on at least every 6th treatment day or no less than once a month.  A 
licensed PT must hold documented conferences with the physical therapy assistant regarding the patient at 
least every 6th treatment day or every 30 days, whichever comes first.   
6. Strategies used to adjust staffing in times of vacancies, emergency or temporary workload increase includes 
but are not limited to the following: 
• Transfer of staff from one location/cost center to another (cross training) 
• Overtime 
• Consultation with referring physician 
• Use of agency nursing staff 
• Use of group therapy for one hour of treatment per day 
• Rescheduling of therapy on those cases where feasible 
• Prioritized by diagnosis / need 
7. Changes in staffing schedules should be coordinated by each area’s manager/supervisor.
