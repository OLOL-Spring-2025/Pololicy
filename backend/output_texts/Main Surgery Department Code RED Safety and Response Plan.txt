PURPOSE: 
To ensure the safety of patients, visitors, and team members in the event of a fire or to the conditions 
that indicate the presence of a fire. To prepare staff to appropriately respond to the danger fires present 
by addressing aspects of fire prevention, preparedness, control, and education.  To delineate authority, 
responsibilities, and procedures to follow in the event of a fire. 
 
POLICY: 
In the event of a fire, all staff will follow Our Lady of the Lake (OLOL) Regional Medical Center’s 
written Code RED Safety and Response Plan to guide and prepare all in the proper response to fires.  
 
Contractors, students, licensed independent practitioners (LIP), and vendors are to follow the instruction 
of unit manager or designee. 
 
Each OLOL department will have their own Department Specific Code RED Safety and Response Plan. 
 
Each location’s Code RED Safety and Response Plan will be used in conjunction with the Emergency 
Operations Plan (ECEP001) and the OLOL Code RED Safety and Response Plan (ECEP007) to guide 
the preparation and response in the event of a fire.

Specific roles of staff related to Code RED drills or Code RED response 
• The following staff members will clear the egress routes of all stretchers, carts, and any other 
obstacles by relocating these items to the clean equipment room behind OR 3:  Case Tech, 
Surgical Tech, or Designee.   
• The following staff members will ensure all fire doors on the Unit are closed:  Case Tech, 
Surgical Tech, or Designee. 
• The following staff members will assist with moving patients and visitors to safety explaining, 
“The hospital is conducting an emergency exercise to ensure your safety”:  all available staff. 
• The following staff members will prevent people from using the elevators: Case Tech or Surgical 
Tech, or Designee  
• The following staff members will assist those with Limited English Proficiency and those with 
sensory or mobility disabilities: Registered Nurse or Designee 
• The following staff members will ensure all employees are accounted for: Registered Nurse or 
Designee  
• The following staff members will ensure each room is empty.  Case tech or Surgical tech or 
Designee (This position will mark the door/door frame with chalk to indicate the room is empty.) 
 
The location of medical gas shut off valves and who has authority to shut them off 
• The Medical gas shut off valves are located outside of each Operating Room.   
• The nurse manager, charge nurse and anesthesiologist has the authority and may designate the 
authority to any staff member to turn off the medical gas.  
  
Location and proper use of equipment for transporting patients between Fire/Smoke Compartments 
(horizontal and vertical evacuations) 
• Equipment used to transport a patient may include: crash carts, oxygen and portable cardiac 
monitors.  
• A crash cart (adult and pediatrics) is located in Core B and an adult crash cart is located in Core 
C.   
• Patients may be under anesthetics or spinals, which would require the staff to transport these 
patients.  
• All patients will be non-ambulatory.  For Horizontal evacuation, we will use stretchers or beds.   
For vertical evacuations we will use the best resource available depending on the patient’s 
needs.   
   
Location and proper use of fire-fighting equipment and manual pull stations. 
• Fire extinguishers are located in every OR, in the hall outside OR 6, in the main hall leading to 
the OR desk, at the OR desk, in the hall outside ORs 1 and 3, and in Cores A & B.    
• Manual pull stations for Main OR are outside the stairwell door next to TNCC waiting room.
