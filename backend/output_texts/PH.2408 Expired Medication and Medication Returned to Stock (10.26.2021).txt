SCOPE: 
Applies to team members who work in or for Our Lady of the Lake Hospital, Inc. (OLOLH) retail/community 
pharmacies. 
PURPOSE: 
To determine the method of disposal of medication that expired prior to dispensing. 
POLICY: 
A. The Pharmacy will protect the safety of its patients by not allowing medication that has left the pharmacy to be 
returned to stock 
B. The staff of the pharmacy will return medication back into stock that has been safely stored in the pharmacy and 
is available to dispense to a different patient. 
C. No expired medication will be dispensed to patients. 
 
DEFINITIONS: 
Dispensing – medication that has been filled and checked by a pharmacist and picked up by patient or designee. 
 
Possession of the Pharmacy- medication stored within the custody of the pharmacy prior to dispensing to a patient or 
designee. 
 
PROCEDURE: 
A. Medications will be stored in the pharmacy until dispensed to a patient or designee. 
B. Medications will not be accepted back into stock once they have left the possession of the pharmacy. [URAC 
PHARM-OP 9 (d-i)] 
C. Filled medications will be stored in the pharmacy prior to dispensing for a period not to exceed 10 business days. 
D. After 10 business days, labels will be removed from unopened manufacturer stock containers if the lot number 
and expiration is visible and placed back in stock for dispensing. 
E. After 10 business days, medications that have been removed from manufacturer stock bottles will be returned to 
stock only if lot number and expiration date not to exceed 6 months from date of fill are written on the label. 
[URAC PHARM-OP 9 (d-ii)]

F. Medications that do not have lot numbers or expiration dates will be quarantined in a container labeled “for 
disposal by reverse distributer” until the next appointment by a contracted reverse distributer. [URAC PHARM-
OP 8 (c)] 
G. Technicians will inspect all stocked medications monthly and pull any medications with expiration dates less 
than 60 days. [URAC PHARM-OP 8 (c)] 
H. Medications noted in section F and G above will be returned for disposal and credit, if applicable, by a licensed 
reverse distributer on a recurring basis no less than 4 times annually  
I. Biohazard compliance for disposal meets state, NCQA and URAC expectations.
