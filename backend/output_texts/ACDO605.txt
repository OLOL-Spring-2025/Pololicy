PURPOSE:  
This policy ensured the appropriate therapist/patient ratios are established in the Acute Care Physical Medicine  
Department. 
 
DEFINITIONS: 
None 
 
POLICY:  
It is the policy of the Acute Care Physical Medicine Department that the appropriate therapist/patient  
staffing ratios are established so that quality patient care is provided by the Acute Care Physical  
Medicine Department. 
 
PROCEDURE: 
 
1.  In general, the therapist’s caseload should consist of the following: 
 Acute: 10-12 patients per day  
2. Support staff shall be used in all areas as needed. 
3. Support staff shall in no way treat patients but may assist a therapist during a patient treatment. 
4. Supervision Ratios for PT, OT, and ST will follow and adhere to their respective licensure rules and Practice 
Acts. 
5. Strategies used to adjust staffing in times of vacancies, emergency or temporary work load increase includes 
but are not limited to the following: 
• Transfer of staff from one location/cost center to another (cross training) 
• Overtime 
• Use of TEN pay with director’s approval 
• Decrease frequency of therapy 
• Prioritized by diagnosis/need or modified plan of care 
• Float staff between Acute, Rehab, and Children's Hospital as needed.  Staffing adjustments must be 
coordinated through each area’s manager/supervisor
