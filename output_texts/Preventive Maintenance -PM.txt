1.PURPOSE -- SCOPE
It is the policy of TRIMEDX that all devices managed by TRIMEDX will have 
an Operation Verification Procedure (OVP) Performance Assurance 
performed prior to initial use and have Preventive Maintenance at periodic 
intervals as defined by an assigned Preventive Maintenance (PM) Schedule 
based on Risk Evaluations, Regulatory Requirements, Contractual 
Requirements or Manufacturer's Recommendations.

3.DEFINITIONS
3.1.(100%) Managed PM - Sum of Timely PM Completion + CNL + In Patient 
Use + Out to Vendor (Documentation of all of these activities in the 
CMMS are required in order to meet this standard).
3.2.Could Not Locate - Type of equipment work order task status where the 
equipment is unable to be located at the site. Work Order Task 
documentation is required. (Refer to Policy Equipment Could Not Locate 
(CNL) for required Service)
3.3.In Patient Use - Type of work order task status where the equipment is in 
patient use. Work Order Task document is required. (Refer to Policy In 
Patient Use (IPU) for required servce)
3.4.Operational Verification Procedure - Operational Verification Procedure 
(OVP) Performance Assurance performed prior to initial use

5.RESPONSIBILITIES
5.1.TRIMEDX Site Leadership
5.1.1.Verify that an appropriate PM Schedule has been assigned to each 
device
5.1.2.Determine if the assigned PM Schedules are appropriate for each 
device (refer to PM Schedule Assignment policy/procedure)
5.1.3.Ensure appropriate documentation submitted when requesting 
changes of the assigned PM Schedules (Refer to policy/procedure 
PM Interval Change).
5.1.4.Ensure that discrepancies with assigned PM Schedules are 
resolved in an expeditious manner
5.1.5.When changes are made to the PM schedule, ensure the device 
PM stickers are updated as well to reflect the new PM schedule due 
date
5.1.6.Ensure that PM work order tasks are adequately updated to 
includes relevant notes about the work completed prior to closing the 
work order
5.1.7.Verify that quality of data such as time entries, close questions, 
service notes, and labor lines are correctly entered and selected prior 
to closing work order.
5.1.8.Ensure that technicians performing the work are qualified to do the 
work
5.1.9.Ensure that PM Work Orders are appropriately closed and closed in 
timely manner
5.1.10.When able to, the site leader should communicate upcoming 
PMs to the client to align on equipment availability

the close questions, and document the steps and results from various 
PM outputs; if applicable, exception reporting is allowed

generated PM Work Orders Tasks each month.  PMs are due by the last 
day of the month for which the system has auto-generated. PM Work 
Order Tasks are generated for each frequency and procedure.
6.2.Preventive Maintenance Work Orders/Work Order Tasks are required to 
be 100% Managed for all medical equipment. The PM is considered

6.3.PM Work Order Tasks will be assigned on the first calendar day of the

6.5.If the device is being used in patient treatment during scheduled 
service: 
6.5.1. Use the 'In Patient Use' button on the WOT until the service can 
actually be completed.
6.5.2. Follow policy/procedure In Patient Use
6.5.3. Service notes will clearly describe the reason that the device is 
unavailable for scheduled service, and what efforts were made to 
correct the situation. Effort should be continuously made and 
documented to receive the device as soon as it is available.
6.5.4. Labor hours and service notes describing the labor activities should 
be added.
6.5.5. Follow scheduled inspection process when equipment becomes 
available.
6.6.If device cannot be located: 
6.6.1. Use the 'Could Not Locate' button on the WOT to indicate the 
device as CNL.
6.6.2. Follow policy/procedure Could Not Locate and document all efforts 
to locate the equipment.
6.6.3. Labor hours and service notes describing the labor activities should 
be added each time an attempt is made to locate the device.
6.6.4. Follow scheduled inspection process when equipment is located.
6.7.If the equipment has been dispositioned: 
6.7.1. Follow policy/procedure Disposition of Equipment; Work Order Flow 
for Disposition of Equipment.
6.7.2. SM approves the disposition WOT.
6.7.3. Technician appropriately cancels the PM WOT by selecting 
Incomplete WOT.

6.9.Delayed Work Order Status 
6.9.1. In the event that the work order task cannot be completed due to a 
delay; you may select the ‘Delayed’ check box on the WOT.
6.9.2. Once checked, you are required to answer, ‘What is Delayed?’: 
6.9.2.1. Parts
6.9.2.2. Vendor Service
6.9.2.3. Site (Customer)
6.9.3. If there is an additional reason that they are delayed, then the 
option for ‘Delay Reason’ is available.
6.9.4. Delayed Work Order Status is not used for any metrics or 
standardized reporting. This is only to help provide reasoning behind 
the work order task not being completed.
6.10.If the PM was previously completed within the PM Schedule's 
window: 
6.10.1. If the PM has not been completed within the month it has 
generated, the PM WOT should not be closed as 'Completed' until the 
work has been done.
6.10.2. If the PM was completed in the past, then PM WOT should 
not be closed as completed to reference a date in the past. Please 
contact Quality and Regulatory if this situation arises.
