LBA075 
 
LABORATORY COMMUNICATION 
 
PURPOSE 
To identify acceptable means of laboratory communication and “hand off” procedures. 
 
 
POLICY 
Effective communication of laboratory operations shall be provided to all employees in  
the most efficient manner possible. 
 
 
PROCEDURE 
Laboratory Communication can be accomplished by a number of procedures: 
• Email communication utilizing “Team Mail” 
• Telephonically 
• Memorandum 
• Formal meetings 
• Review of Press Ga iney Survey results for patient, physician, and employee 
satisfaction scores 
• Review of surveys by various governmental and accrediting agencies which may 
include, but are not limited to: FDA, OSHA, Louisiana State Fire Marshall, DEQ, 
DHH, CAP, COLA, AABB and TJC. 
 
“Hand Off’ procedures may include: 
• Charge tech- responsible for reporting to relief charge tech or supervisor.  Must 
ensure pertinent information is communicated to all incoming staff. 
• Section Huddle Meetings with staff 
• Review of section problems/concerns with oncoming staff 
• Review pending list(s) during shift, and with relief staff 
• Individual in -person communication, which should include a thorough relay of 
pertinent information relating to patient care and acuity of testing
