1.PURPOSE -- SCOPE
TRIMEDX will comply with TRIMEDX and the Facility's policies and 
procedures when managing hazard alerts and equipment recalls for 
TRIMEDX covered equipment.
2.REFERENCE DOCUMENTS
3.DEFINITIONS
3.1.Affected? Yes - Condition/issue addressed in the alert pertains to the 
equipment and can be rectified by the TRIMEDX Technician or Site 
Manager
3.2.Affected? No - Condition/issue addressed in the alert does not pertain to 
the equipment or equipment is not affected
3.3.Alert Review Committee - The Alert Review Committee will be 
comprised of the Managers, Quality & Regulatory, the Vice President, 
Quality & Regulatory, the Divisional Vice President(s), and 
representatives from the following departments: Supply Chain, Supplier 
Quality. Additionally, the Vice President of Supply Chain and the Chief 
Operating Officer will be invited to join.
3.4.Anticipated Service Provider – In House - Condition/issue addressed in 
the alert pertains to the equipment and can be resolved by the technician 
or manager in house.
3.5.Anticipated Service Provider – Manufacturer - Condition/issue 
addressed in the alert pertains to the equipment but needs to be resolved 
by the Manufacturer (waiting on parts, documents or service)
3.6.Medical device alert:  Work order that is created by the QRC team in 
response to a potential medical device safety advisory that requires 
potential action.
3.7.Medical device recall:  Work order that is created by the QRC team in 
response to potential medical device safety recall that requires immediate 
action and removal of the device from patient use.  
4.ABBREVIATIONS
4.1.CNL - Could Not Locate; equipment could not be found
4.2.CO - Corporate Office
4.3.FDA- Food and Drug Administration

5.RESPONSIBILITIES
5.1.TRIMEDX Managers
5.1.1. Communicate and provide Alert/Recall notice and relevant 
documentation to hospital and QRC Department
5.1.2. Maintain a log of proper documentation of hazard and recall notices
5.1.3. Ensure that appropriate investigation is conducted based on the 
Alert WO/WOT opened prior to approval
5.1.4. Review all Alert Work Orders/WOTs to ensure appropriate 
intervention and documentation prior to approval
5.1.5. Submit Bulk Update Alerts Requests to QRC Department if 
applicable
5.1.6. Report any actions taken at the Facility's monthly regulatory/safety 
meetings and report any Adverse Patient Outcomes at Safety
5.1.7. Meetings and to TRIMEDX Corporate
5.1.8. Ensure Business Reply Form attached to the alert (or provided by 
manufacturer) is accurately filled out and is returned to 
manufacturer.
5.2.TRIMEDX Managed Technicians
5.2.1. Locate the device addressed in the Alert/Recall WOT
5.2.2. Verify that the problem identified in the Alert/Recall applies to the 
device
5.2.3. Accurately fill out Alert/Recall WOT with the required information
5.2.4. Leave all Alert/Recall WOTs open until they are resolved
5.2.5. Address Alert/Recall WOTs within 30 days of receipt of the notice

5.2.6. Identify and monitor Alert/Recall WOTs that require Manufacturer 
Service or will remain open past 90 days
5.3.QRC Department
5.3.1. Perform an inventory search of RSQ to identify devices possibly 
affected by the hazard alert or equipment recall received from 
TRIMEDX Managers or from the FDA
5.3.2. Open Alert/Recall WO/WOTs for all equipment possibly affected by 
the alert for equipment less than 1,000 in quantity
5.3.3. Escalate Alert/Recall to Vice President, Quality & Regulatory and 
Manager, QRC Department if the equipment count is 10,000 + or 
averaging 50 devices per site in quantity, and/or parts cost could be 
deemed high based upon WO/WOT quantity. The Alert Review 
Committee will be initiated in these scenarios to determine next 
steps and provide visibility to Senior Leadership
5.3.4. Provide a copy of the hazard alert or equipment recall to the 
affected sites
5.3.5. Complete Bulk Update Alerts Requests
5.3.6. Monitor open Alert/Recall WO/WOTs and provide Compliance 
Reports to TRIMEDX Leadership
5.3.7. Review closed Alert/Recall WO/WOTs periodically to ensure 
appropriate actions and documentation
6.PROCEDURE
6.1.SM receives Alert/Recall notice from Manufacturer
6.2.SM provides a copy of hazard alert and equipment recalls to the 
responsible hospital staff.
6.3.SM provides a copy (prefer: electronic) of hazard alerts and equipment 
recalls to the QRC Department at the CO as a Regulatory Request.
6.4.QRC determines if the impact of the alert work orders will exceed 1,000 in 
quantity. If so, the alert is escalated to the Vice President, Quality & 
Regulatory and Managers, Quality & Regulatory prior to any actions being 
taken. The Vice President and Managers will initiate a meeting with the

6.14.Within 30 days, all alerts should be, at a minimum, addressed 
(Remediation Underway Sub-status) with an Affected? field completed.
6.15. Completed Business Reply forms in the alert should be sent back to the 
manufacturer or provided to Hospital Alert management representative.
6.16. Not Affected alerts should be closed
6.17. Affected alerts that required Manufacturer service:
6.17.1.SM schedules service directly with the manufacturer
6.17.2. Open past 90 days should be monitored (contact Mfr/vendor 
on status) and notes entered every 90 days.
6.18. Affected alerts that can be serviced by in-house staff:
6.18.1. Should be closed as soon as possible
6.18.2. Open past 90 days should be monitored and notes entered 
every 30 days until resolved.
6.19. Delayed Work Order Status
6.19.1. In the event that the work order task cannot be completed due to a 
delay; you may select the ‘Delayed’ check box on the WOT.
6.19.2. Once checked, you are required to answer, ‘What is Delayed?’:
6.19.2.1. Parts
6.19.2.2. Vendor Service
6.19.2.3. Site (Customer)
6.19.3. If there is an additional reason that they are delayed, then 
the option for ‘Delay Reason’ is available.
6.19.4. Delayed Work Order Status is not used for any metrics or 
standardized reporting. This is only to help provide reasoning 
behind the work order task not being completed.
6.20.SM/Technician can request a bulk update of Alert WO/WOT 
Notes, Affected?, Close Fields, and labor lines by submitting the following 
to QRC in a Bulk Update Alerts request in RSQ
6.21.Filter your WOT list to Alert WOTs to be updated, filtering to one 
reference number at a time.
6.22.Once satisfied with the filtered list, go to the Related Links at the bottom 
and click Bulk Update Alerts.
6.23.Complete the Bulk Update of Alert Work Order Tasks form
6.24.Answer the question of Affected?
