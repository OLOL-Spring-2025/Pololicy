PURPOSE: 
To ensure the safety of patients, visitors, and team members in the event of a fire or to the conditions 
that indicate the presence of a fire. To prepare staff to appropriately respond to the danger fires present 
by addressing aspects of fire prevention, preparedness, control, and education.  To delineate authority, 
responsibilities, and procedures to follow in the event of a fire. 
 
POLICY: 
In the event of a fire, all staff will follow Our Lady of the Lake (OLOL) Regional Medical Center’s 
written Code RED Safety and Response Plan to guide and prepare all in the proper response to fires.  
 
Contractors, students, licensed independent practitioners (LIP), and vendors are to follow the instruction 
of unit manager or designee. 
 
Each OLOL department will have their own Department Specific Code RED Safety and Response Plan. 
 
Each location’s Code RED Safety and Response Plan will be used in conjunction with the Emergency 
Operations Plan (ECEP001) and the OLOL Code RED Safety and Response Plan (ECEP007) to guide 
the preparation and response in the event of a fire.

Specific roles of staff related to Code RED drills or Code RED response: 
• Case Tech or designee will clear the hallway of any carts or equipment by moving them to 
Supply Room. 
• Case Tech, Orderly or designee will close doors and windows. 
• Charge Nurse or designee will confirm the automatic fire doors closed. 
•  RN Circulator, CRNA. Surgical Technician or designee will explain to patients and visitors, 
“The hospital is conducting an emergency exercise to ensure your safety”. 
• Scheduler or designee will also not be in a surgical suite and can provide assistance with 
ensuring no one uses the elevator.  
• RN Circulator, CRNA or Surgical Technician will assist those with Limited English Proficiency 
(LEP) and those with sensory or mobility disabilities. 
• Charge Nurse or designee will ensure all employees are accounted for. 
• Manager/Supervisor or designee will ensure all patients are accounted for. 
• Manager/Supervisor or designee will ensure each room is empty. 
 
The location of medical gas shut off valves and who has authority to shut them off 
• Medical gas shut off valves are location on each hallway in Tower Surgery department 
• The nurse manager, charge nurse and anesthesiologist has the authority and may designate the 
authority to any staff member to turn off the medical gas 
 
Location and proper use of equipment for transporting patients between Fire/Smoke Compartments 
(horizontal and vertical evacuations) 
• Equipment used to transports patients may include:  crash carts, oxygen, and portable cardiac 
monitors.  
• Each sterile core has a crash cart (adult and peds) and two portable cardiac monitors.   
• Patients may be under anesthetics or spinals, which would require the staff to transport these 
patients.  
• All patients will be non-ambulatory.  For Horizontal evacuation we will use stretchers.  For 
Vertical evacuation we will use the best resource available depending on the patient’s needs. 
 
Location and proper use of fire-fighting equipment and manual pull stations. 
• One fire extinguisher on each hall way, in every Operating Room, one in each sterile core 
• Fire pulls are located at the nursing station and at the end of the hallway past Room 11. 
 
Anything that makes the department unique from a life safety standpoint.  
• Oxygen to the Unit and any device using oxygen (anesthesia machine).  
• Surgical patients under anesthetics may need staff to physically move them on beds or stretchers. 
• All Lake Employees with a valid badge can get into the surgical department.
