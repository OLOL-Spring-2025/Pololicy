PURPOSE:  
This policy outlines the Process for thorough documentation of patient progress notes shall be written to include  
patient progress in relation to treatment plan/goals for patients of the Acute Care Physical Medicine Department. 
 
DEFINITIONS: 
None 
 
POLICY:  
 
It is the policy of the Acute Physical Medicine Department that Progress notes shall be written to include patient  
progress in relation to treatment plan/goals. 
 
 
PROCEDURE: 
1. Each treatment rendered shall be documented on the patient’s electronic medical record.  Progress 
notes/treatment documentation are written at each treatment session.  Notes shall be recorded by a qualified, 
licensed therapist or therapist assistant. 
2. Progress/Treatment documentation notes must reflect progress or regression.  If they reflect regression, 
documentation must show evidence of the cause of regression. 
3. Treatment refusals and the reason for refusal, as well as patient unavailability must be documented in the 
electronic medical record 
4. Objective findings shall be regularly recorded, such as range of motion, strength, endurance, and assistance 
required for ADL’s including transfers, functional mobility, etc…. 
5. Documentation addresses treatment plan changes.  Only a licensed therapist may document initial assessment, 
additional orders, or changes in treatment plan.
