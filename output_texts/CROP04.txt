Purpose: 
 
To establish guidelines for the document required to assure that the patient has been properly informed about the 
Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation Program and voluntarily agrees, or consents, to participate. 
 
Applies To:                      All Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation Staff 
 
Policy: 
 
All Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation patients/participants will give voluntary, documented consent to participate, 
prior to beginning the Program. 
 
The consent form will explain the Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation Program, monitoring of the exercise sessions, 
risks and discomforts commonly associated with exercise, benefits to be expected by participation, responsibility 
of the participant, alternatives, use of any medical records, and freedom of consent.  The Cardiopulmonary 
Rehabilitation team will answer any  questions prior to the patient signing the consent form. 
 
A member of the Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation Staff will review the form with the patient.  All questions will 
be answered to the patient's satisfaction.  Both parties will sign and date the form in agreement to what it states. 
 
Procedure: 
 
The consent form may be reviewed and signed at any time prior to the beginning of the program by the patient. 
Usually, the consent form will be introduced to the patient as part of the initial assessment/ orientation to the 
Cardiac or Pulmonary Rehabilitation program.
