PURPOSE: 
To define criteria for performing Activated Clotting time (ACT) at the patient’s bedside. 
 
Addresses: 
Supervisors 
Venipuncture Charge Techs 
Venipuncture Phlebotomists 
Medical Laboratory Assistants 
 
POLICY: 
A. Timely performance of the test is important to the care of the patient 
B. ACT testing is a STAT test irregardless of actual order priority 
C. Timed requests for ACT testing are to be performed as requested 
D. The Charge Tech is to notify the patient’s nurse of unexpected delays in performing patient testing 
E. The person performing the test is to be licensed as a Louisiana: 
a. Laboratory Assistant 
b. Medical Laboratory Technician 
c. Medical Laboratory Technologist 
PROCEDURE: 
A. Retrieve the Hemochron Signature Elite meter and test cuvette from the Venipuncture Department. 
B. Take the instrument to the patient’s care area 
C. Perform proper Patient Identification (refer to Patient Identification Procedure) 
D. Perform the ACT as described in the “Point of Care Whole Blood Activated Clotting Time” Procedure 
E. Report the ACT Test result to the patient’s nurse 
F. Write the ACT test result in whole seconds on the laboratory label along with the time of testing and your 
Network ID identifying you as the person performing the test 
G. Send the laboratory label to the Central Processing area of the laboratory 
H. Return the Hemochron Signature Elite meter to the Venipuncture Department 
I. Plug the meter to its power source to keep the battery ready for the next use 
J. Plug the meter into the network cable to electronically transfer the results from the Hemochron Signature 
Elite meter to the patient’s EMR. OUR LADY OF THE LAKE RMC
