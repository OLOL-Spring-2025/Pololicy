POLICY: 
 
The Pharmacy Department Leader or designee shall ensure the availability of a sufficient inventory of 
medical staff approved emergency medications in the pharmacy and patient care areas. 
 
AVAILABILITY OF EMERGENCY MEDICATIONS 
Emergency medications shall be readily available to the patient-care staff but not accessible to patients, 
visitors, and unauthorized personnel. 
 
RESTRICTED USE OF EMERGENCY MEDICATIONS 
Emergency medications supplies shall not be used as a routine or stat source but shall be reserved for 
emergency use when immediate availability is necessary. 
 
SELECTION OF MEDICATIONS 
Emergency medications shall be appropriate to the needs of the patient-care area. 
 
The medical staff shall approve the kinds and quantities of medications.  A list of the approved medications 
shall be maintained. 
 
CONTAINERS 
Emergency medication containers (e.g., trays, kits, boxes, and crash carts) shall be prepared in consultation 
with the medical staff. 
 
ARRANGEMENT OF MEDICATIONS 
Emergency medication containers shall be arranged to facilitate rapid implementation of emergency care.  PH-03-29

MAINTENANCE OF CONTAINERS 
The pharmacy shall maintain medications in the containers at predetermined (par) levels. 
 
SYSTEMS TO ASSURE SECURITY AND INTEGRITY OF EMERGENCY MEDICATIONS 
The pharmacy may use a breakable seal or other system designed to assure the continued security and 
integrity of the contents between periods of use. 
 
CONTROL OF SEALS 
Seals, if used, shall be controlled by the pharmacy and shall not be available to nursing service and others 
who use the medications.  An  appropriately locked container with a tamper proof seal and earliest 
medication expiration date visible is sufficient to determine that medications are present and usable. 
 
A record of user inspections shall include: 
• The inspection date 
• The location of the emergency medications 
• The signature of the inspector 
 
CONTROLLING EMERGENCY MEDICATION SUPPLIES -- INSPECTION PROCEDURE 
The pharmacy shall establish a monitoring and inspection system to ensure the integrity and ready 
availability of emergency medication supplies. 
 
REPLACEMENT OF MEDICATIONS 
The pharmacy shall replace missing, expired, and unusable medications as soon as possible. 
 
FINAL CHECK OF EMERGENCY MEDICATIONS 
The pharmacy shall perform a final check of emergency medications and, if applicable, seal the container.
