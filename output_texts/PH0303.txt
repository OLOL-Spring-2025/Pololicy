POLICY: 
Specifications and selection of blood products used are the responsibilities of the physician Medical 
Director of the Laboratory.  Blood derivatives managed by the pharmacy shall be controlled, procured, 
stored, distributed, administered, and monitored as described in this policy. 
CONTROL OF BLOOD DERIVATIVES 
The Pharmacy  Department leader  or designee shall control the procurement, storage, distribution, 
administration, and monitoring of blood derivatives managed by the pharmacy. 
PROCUREMENT 
The pharmacy shall order blood derivatives only from suppliers who meet the standards described in  
pharmacy policies and procedures. 
STORAGE 
The pharmacy shall ensure that blood derivatives are stored in accordance with the recommendations of 
the manufacturer/distributor. 
DISTRIBUTION 
The pharmacy shall supply blood derivatives only upon the order of a practitioner.  Blood products may 
be stored and dispensed from the automated dispensing machine and operating room satellite. 
ADMINISTRATION 
Blood derivatives shall be administered only by an individual who is permitted by law and medical staff 
rules and regulations to administer them.  The administration of the product shall be recorded in the 
patientâ€™s medical record. 
MONITORING 
Persons who receive blood derivatives shall be monitored to ensure that their condition warrants continued 
use of the product.
