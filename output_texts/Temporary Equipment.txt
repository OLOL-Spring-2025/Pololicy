1.PURPOSE -- SCOPE
To define process for management of temporary equipment through all 
necessary steps from entry into facility to removal.
It is the policy of TRIMEDX that all equipment managed or serviced by TRIMEDX 
is accurately inventoried and entered into the CMMS as appropriate.
NOTE: TRIMEDX does not endorse nor recommend the use of patient-owned 
equipment within any medical facility. If local policy dictates its use, patient 
owned equipment will be managed in accordance with the policy of each facility.
2.REFERENCE DOCUMENTS
3.DEFINITIONS
3.1.Temporary Equipment: Equipment brought into the facility and intended 
for short-term use. Typically, the length of time equipment resides in 
facility is not to exceed duration of one Alternative Equipment 
Management (AEM) PM cycle or one year. Temporary equipment can be 
classified as loaner, rental, demo, patient-owned or physician-owned.
3.1.1.Loaner: Medical equipment that is provided under an arrangement 
based on lending, normally provided by a manufacturer or vendor and 
typically for short-term use
3.1.2.Rental: Medical equipment that is provided under an arrangement 
based on rent-for-use and typically for short-term use
3.1.3.Trial: Medical equipment that is provided under an arrangement 
based on trying, testing or demonstrating, normally provided by a 
manufacturer or vendor and typically for short-term use
3.1.4.Patient-owned: Equipment brought into the facility by the patient 
for the patient's use and not owned by the facility. Examples - Patient-
owned medical equipment such as CPAP's, BiPAPs, and Ventilators, 
etc. Patient owned personal equipment such as Laptops, curling 
irons, fans, hair dryers, etc.

5.1.TRIMEDX Technicians
5.1.1. Will perform a visual inspection and an electrical safety inspection 
(ESI) on all temporary equipment prior to clinical use:
5.1.1.1. In the case of a two-pronged device where an ESI cannot be 
done, a visual inspection will take place to ensure the physical 
integrity of the device and the power cord and to confirm it is a 
double insulated device.
5.1.2. Will place the appropriate TRIMEDX label(s) on all temporary 
equipment in the proper location;
5.1.3. Are responsible for timely, accurate, and appropriate entry and 
retirement of temporary equipment included in the CMMS;
5.1.3.1. Verifies that temporary equipment entered into the CMMS 
passes the incoming inspection and is appropriately 
documented;
5.1.3.2. Assigns appropriate Temporary CEID, and Temporary 
device status/substatus in the CMMS for temporary equipment; 
and
5.1.4. Locates temporary equipment upon work order task generation or 
during annual inventory revalidation to determine status/disposition 
and updates CMMS appropriately
5.2.TRIMEDX Site Leadership
5.2.1. Will ensure completion of ESI and appropriate labeling on all 
temporary equipment prior to clinical use;
5.2.2. Responsible for ensuring timely, accurate, and appropriate entry 
and disposition of temporary equipment included in the CMMS 
database;
5.2.2.1. Provides timely review and approval of all temporary 
equipment incoming inventory and disposition work orders;
5.2.2.2. Verifies that all temporary equipment in CMMS has an 
appropriate Temporary CEID and Temporary status;

6.PROCEDURE
6.1.Clinical/Biomedical Engineering is notified of temporary equipment 
received into the facility for clinical use.
6.2.If equipment is patient-owned: Refer to local hospital policy if a local 
policy exists. If not, use this policy as the primary guide. Perform an 
electrical safety inspection (ESI) (refer to acceptable exception noted 
under TRIMEDX Technicians Responsibilities section above) and tag 
equipment with TRIMEDX "Approved for Use" label. If hospital policy 
dictates entry into CMMS database, follow the "All other temporary 
devices" instructions below.

6.3.If equipment will be in facility for a few hours or less than one day: 
Perform a visual inspection and an ESI (unless it is a two-prong device, 
which warrants visual inspection of the cord to make sure it is double 
insulated and intact) , tag equipment with TRIMEDX "Approved for Use" 
label, document and track on a Service Request WOT (see Performance 
Verification Policy).
6.3.1. The device should be tracked to ensure the device has left within 
the same day that it arrived. If the device crosses over 24 hours, then 
the equipment should be added to inventory (see Performance 
Verification Policy)
6.4.All other temporary equipment: Enter into the CMMS database as 
outlined per TRIMEDX Incoming Inventory Policy/Process with the 
following exceptions:
6.4.1. Enter the following via the RSQ Incoming Inventory Wizard:
6.4.1.1. Enter CEID as 'T' + Model Name/Serial Number(I.E. 
TFP5000/105999);
6.4.1.2. Select Equipment Status as "Temporary";
6.4.1.3. Select Substatus as either "Loaner, Rental, Patient-Owned 
or Demo";
6.4.1.4. Enter Actual Cost $0.00;
6.4.1.5. Under the Coverage and Strategy Tab
6.4.1.5.1. Enter 'None' under Warranty Type
6.4.1.5.2. Delete the dates in Warranty Start Date and Warranty 
End Date
6.4.2. Complete information on TRIMEDX Temporary Device sticker and 
place on equipment.
