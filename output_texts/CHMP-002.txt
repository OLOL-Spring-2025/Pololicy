PURPOSE: 
 
Standardize the procedure for medication ordering for patients admitted to the Children’s Hospital. 
 
POLICY: 
 
1. Computerized physician order entry (CPOE) medication orders for patients <50kg, should be entered using 
the dosage equation (mg/kg or mg/m2).  Rounding calculator can be used to round dose as appropriate. 
2. Weight must be recorded in the electronic medical record prior to medication ordering. 
3. Children’s Hospital order sets should be used when available. 
4. All medications should have an indication noted.  This can be in the physician’s notes or as a comment on 
the medication order. 
5. For multi-drug combinations the medication order format must be applied to at least one component. 
6. Standard dosing exceptions: heparin line flushes, ophthalmic preparations, otic drops, nasal sprays, topical 
preparations, aerosol treatments, metered dose inhalers, glycerin suppositories, thrombolytics for declotting 
central lines or chest tubes, pancreatic enzymes, vitamins, immunizations. 
7. Home medications that are being continued without changes do not require a dose equation but should have 
a notation of the indication. 
8. If all elements of medication order are not complete, the pharmacist will contact the physician to obtain the 
missing information. 
  
9. In the event that medication orders are written for the pediatric patients <50kg the following format must be 
used: 
a. Drug 
b. Total dose 
c. Dose equation (mg/kg/day or mg/kg/dose or mg/m2/dose) 
d. Route 
e. Frequency
