PURPOSE: 
To ensure the safety of patients, visitors, and team members in the event of a fire or to the conditions 
that indicate the presence of a fire. To prepare staff to appropriately respond to the danger fires present 
by addressing aspects of fire prevention, preparedness, control, and education.  To delineate authority, 
responsibilities, and procedures to follow in the event of a fire. 
 
POLICY: 
In the event of a fire, all staff will follow the Our Lady of the Lake Children’s Hospital (OLOLCH) 
written Code RED Safety and Response Plan to guide and prepare all in the proper response to fires.  
 
Contractors, students, licensed independent practitioners (LIP), and vendors are to follow the instruction 
of unit manager or designee. 
 
Each OLOLCH department will have their own Department Specific Code RED Safety and Response 
Plan. 
 
Each location’s Code RED Safety and Response Plan will be used in conjunction with the Emergency 
Operations Plan (ECEP001) and the OLOLCH Code RED Safety and Response Plan (CH-ECEP007) to 
guide the preparation and response in the event of a fire.

Specific roles of staff related to Code RED drills or Code RED response 
Emergency Services: 
• Patient Access Representative (PAR) or Designee will clears the hallway of any carts or 
equipment by moving them to Patient Access Discharge Office 
• PAR or designee will close doors and windows 
• PAR or designee will confirm automatic fire doors closed 
• PAR sitting at Triage or designee will move patients and visitors to safety explaining, “The 
hospital is conducting an emergency exercise to ensure your safety” 
• PAR or designee prevents people from using elevator 
• Patient Access Leadership or designee will assist those with Limited English Proficiency (LEP) 
and those with sensory or mobility disabilities 
• PAR or designee will ensure all employees are accounted for 
• PAR or designee will ensure each room is empty.  
 
Outpatient Radiology: 
• PAR or designee will close doors and windows 
• PAR or designee will confirm automatic fire doors closed 
• PAR or designee will prevent people from using elevator 
• Patient Access Leader or designee will assist those with Limited English Proficiency (LEP) and 
those with sensory or mobility disabilities 
• PAR or designee will ensure all employees are accounted for 
• PAR or designee will ensure each room is empty.  
 
Location and proper use of fire-fighting equipment and manual pull stations. 
• The closest Fire Extinguisher is located outside reception on the Radiology waiting area wall. 
• The closest Manual Pull Station is located outside reception area on the wall outside Stairwell 3. 
 
The location of the horizontal and vertical evacuation routes and destinations. 
Emergency Services: 
• The primary horizontal evacuation route will be through the Emergency Department entrance 
gathering at the back of the Public Parking area. 
• The secondary horizontal evacuation route will be through the double doors into the Emergency 
Department. 
 
Outpatient Radiology: 
• The primary horizontal evacuation route will be through the front doors of the hospital gathering 
at the back of the Public Parking area. 
• The secondary horizontal evacuation route will be through the double doors into Imaging. 
 
• Admissions will only have horizontal evacuation.
