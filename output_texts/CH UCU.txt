PURPOSE: 
To ensure the safety of patients, visitors, and team members in the event of a fire or to the conditions 
that indicate the presence of a fire. To prepare staff to appropriately respond to the danger fires present 
by addressing aspects of fire prevention, preparedness, control, and education.  To delineate authority, 
responsibilities, and procedures to follow in the event of a fire. 
 
POLICY: 
In the event of a fire, all staff will follow the Our Lady of the Lake Children’s Hospital (OLOLCH) 
written Code RED Safety and Response Plan to guide and prepare all in the proper response to fires.  
 
Contractors, students, licensed independent practitioners (LIP), and vendors are to follow the instruction 
of unit manager or designee. 
 
Each OLOLCH department will have their own Department Specific Code RED Safety and Response 
Plan. 
 
Each location’s Code RED Safety and Response Plan will be used in conjunction with the Emergency 
Operations Plan (ECEP001) and the OLOLCH Code RED Safety and Response Plan (CH-ECEP007) to 
guide the preparation and response in the event of a fire.

Specific roles of staff related to Code RED drills or Code RED response 
• The CNA and/or Nurse will move items out of the hall and into the supply room. 
• The CNA and/or Nurse will close all doors on the unit.  
• The charge nurse or designee will confirm automatic fire doors closed  
• The bedside nurse or designee will move patients and visitors to safety explaining, “The hospital 
is conducting an emergency exercise to ensure your safety”  
• The CNA or Nurse will prevent people from using the elevators 
• The Bedside nurse or CNA will assist those with Limited English Proficiency (LEP) and those 
with sensory or mobility disabilities   
• The Charge nurse or designee will ensure all employees are accounted for.   
• The Charge nurse or designee will ensures all patients are accounted for  
• The Charge nurse or designee will ensure each room is empty.   
• The Charge nurse or designee will confirm Area of Refuge occupants or those needing assistance 
have been evacuated. 
 
The location of medical gas shut off valves and who has authority to shut them off 
• The medical gas shut off valves are located outside the Procedure Room and outside PICU Room 
21. 
 
Location and proper use of equipment for transporting patients between Fire/Smoke 
Compartments (horizontal and vertical evacuations) 
• The Patient bed, crib, or bassinet depending on age of patient or 2 small wheelchairs will be used 
to transport the patients off of the unit.  The wheelchairs are located in Equipment Storage. 
• UCU will require extra assistance moving ventilated patients.   
 
Location and proper use of fire-fighting equipment and manual pull stations. 
• Fire extinguishers are located outside the Meds Room, near the double doors entering the shell 
space, and inside the Data and Electric rooms. 
• The nearest Manual Pull Station is located outside Stairwell 7. 
 
Anything that makes the department unique from a life safety standpoint.  
• Oxygen tanks are located in the treatment room. 
• Ventilated patients and bed bound patients will need extra assistance being moved if needed. 
• The entire Children’s hospital requires badge access.   
 
The location of the horizontal and vertical evacuation routes and destinations. 
• The primary horizontal evacuation route is through the double doors toward Respiratory or 
through the double doors entering hall toward the Visitor Elevators. 
• The primary vertical evacuation route is through double doors toward the Visitor Elevators down 
Stairwell 3 into the Public Parking Area.
