POLICY: 
All nurses are to give verbal shift report to the nurse assigned to care for those patients on the 
oncoming shift utilizing the SBAR process.  Communication should allow for interactive 
participation and the ability to question or clarify information. 
 
PROCEDURES: 
The nurse reporting off duty should be able to give a summary report of those patients he/she 
cared for on his/her shift.  This report should include all pertinent data but should focus on 
abnormal findings in the review of systems assessment. Bedside Shift Report should be done on 
non-Mental Behavioral Health Units. Report should include the following SBAR elements: 
  
SITUATION/BACKGROUND: 
• Identify self (person giving report). 
• Identify patient’s name, physician, diagnosis, pertinent history (i.e. physical 
and psychosocial), code status, allergies and medical alerts. 
• Concise statement of the problem/chief complaint 
• New orders, treatments, medications, diet, I.V. therapy 
• Social Service or educational needs 
• List PRN meds given and response 
• Overdue tasks 
• Flagged events 
 
ASSESSMENT: 
• Abnormal findings noted in the nursing assessment for previous 8 to 12 hours. 
• Labs, micro, patho, diagnostics 
• I&O 
• Vital Signs 
• Lines, Tubes, and Drains 
 
RECOMMENDATIONS (actions requested/recommended): 
• Current patient problems (nursing diagnosis), plan of care, and progress 
towards goals. 
• Patient/Family Education  
• Discharge/transfer information. 
• Quality Measures
