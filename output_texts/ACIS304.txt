PURPOSE:  
This policy outlines the speech therapy process for artificial larynges evaluation and treatment for inpatients of the 
Acute Care Physical Medicine Department.  
 
DEFINITIONS: 
None 
 
POLICY:  
 
It is the policy of the Acute Physical Medicine Departmentâ€™s Speech Pathology Division to supervise 
the use of artificial larynges with patients following laryngectomy surgery during the hospital stay. 
 
PROCEDURE: 
1. Attending physicians must order Speech Language Pathology (SLP) for evaluation and treatment.  
The physician may state artificial larynx (AL) in the details section of the order.  The patient will be 
provided a personal AL in the laryngectomy pulmonary kit from the OR. 
2. The speech pathologist will use the artificial larynx with the patient during the therapy session. 
3. The speech pathologist will demonstrate proper use of the artificial larynx to the patient and family.   
4. Progress notes will include documentation of progress made with the AL and best placement of use. 
5. The speech pathologist will request continued therapy for further training of the AL when 
appropriate.  In the event a demo AL is used, it will be kept in the speech pathology supply cabinet 
when not in use. 
6. When in storage, the battery should be kept out of the AL in the original box.   
7. The speech pathologist using the AL will be responsible for placement and storage of each unit used. 
8. The AL will be cleaned between each patient use according to appropriate equipment infection 
control standards.
