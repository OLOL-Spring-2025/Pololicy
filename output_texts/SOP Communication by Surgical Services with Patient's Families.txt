SUBJECT: 
Standard Operating Process  
Communication by Surgical Services with Patients' Families 
 
Page 1 of 1  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Purpose:   
To provide guidelines for communication with families on patient's condition during surgery, procedures, and post-
operatively. 
 
Applies To:   
Surgical Services 
 
Policy:   
During the admission phase to surgical services, the pre op nurse will verify the name of the specific next of kin, 
relative, or significant others with the patient that is allowed to have report on their results or procedures taking 
place.   The circulating nurse shall give the family member notification after the case begins, and at least every 1 
hour, and at the end of the case, until the patient is transported to the Post-Anesthesia Care Unit/Critical Care area or 
returned to their room. 
 
Responsibility:  
Surgical Services Personnel 
Procedure:   
1. Upon admit to Surgical Services, the pre-op nurse will ask the patient for the specific name of the person they 
want their physician/surgeon to speak to concerning the results of their procedure or post-operative report.  
This information shall be documented in the patient’s medical record  on the Communication and Safety 
Checklist Form  
2. After induction of anesthesia and the case begins, the circulating nurse calls the Surgery Waiting Room by 
telephone to let the family member know the case has begun. 
3. Call the family at least every 60 minutes to update. 
4.  At the end of the procedure, notify the specified person  of procedure end, and direct them to physician 
consultation area.  
5. Once the patient arrives in PACU, the PACU staff will notify the family member of patient’s condition and the 
approximate time of stay in the PACU unit.  The PACU staff will notify the family if the stay is prolonged. 
6. Notification times are documented in the patient’s medical record. 
 
 
Reference:   
JACHO Standards, 2002.
