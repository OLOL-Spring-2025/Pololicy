Policy: 
 
PROGRESS REPORTS 
Reports will be sent at 12, 24, 36 sessions in the Phase II (Outpatient) Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation 
Program and every 3 months in the Phase III Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation Program unless requested 
otherwise.  Reports will be sent to the cardiologist including an end of program report. 
 
Pulmonary Rehabilitation reports will be sent to the referring physician and/or pulmonologist at 12, 24, 36 
sessions. Reports will be sent to the referring physician, and a discharge report will be sent to the physician 
upon termination of the program. The physician will have the opportunity to recommend continuation in 
Basic Pulmonary Rehabilitation, entrance into Maintenance Level, or discharge from the program upon 
receipt of the progress report. 
 
The Cardiac Rehabilitation Progress Reports will include but not be limited to the following information: 
 
 · Dates covered by report 
 · Number of sessions attended by patient in this report period 
 · Exercise MET levels 
 · Total duration of activities 
 · Lipid Levels (if available) 
 · Weight and body fat (if available)  
 · Duke Activity Status Index estimate and Six Minute Walk Test results 
 · Program goals for each category for the patient 
 · Patient education topics 
 · Comments 
Comments are reported concerning achievement of patient goals, monitoring, how patient is tolerating 
exercise program, patient difficulties or changes in patient condition, including patient comments. 
 
Pulmonary Rehabilitation Progress Reports will include: 
 · Dates covered by report  · Number of sessions attended by patient in this report period 
 · Exercise MET levels 
 · Total duration of activities 
 · Pulse oximetry and supplemental oxygen use 
 · Educational Instruction and comprehension 
 · Rate of Perceived Dyspnea (RPD) during Activities of Daily Living 
 · Duke Activity Status Index estimate and Six Minute Walk Test results 
 · Program goals for each category for the patient 
 · Education Instruction and comprehension 
 · Comments 
Comments are reported concerning achievement of patient goals, monitoring, how patient is tolerating 
exercise program, patient difficulties or changes in patient condition, including patient comments. 
 
PATIENT CONFERENCES  
Patient conferences will be conducted every 12 sessions for all cardiac and pulmonary rehabilitation 
outpatients, with the exception of Maintenance level participants. The conference should include discussion   
concerning attainment of patient goals, education class attendance or instruction, expression of appropriate 
needs concerning the individual's program, or other desires concerning treatment or difficulties which the 
patient may be experiencing.  Family or support individuals are encouraged to attend upon approval of the 
patient. 
 
The conference findings will be reviewed by members of the team and appropriate action will be taken to 
help the patient achieve any program goals which have not yet been attained and to fulfill the patient's 
expressed needs or desires. 
 
Prior to discharge, a patient will also be asked to complete a discharge questionnaire for the purpose of 
evaluating the cardiac or pulmonary rehabilitation program. 
 
Information from all conferences, or discharge questionnaire, may be used to complete progress reports which 
will be sent to the patient's referring physician.
