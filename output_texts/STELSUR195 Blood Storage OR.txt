SUBJECT: 
Blood Storage in the Operating Room 
 
Page 1 of 2 
 
 
Follows Lab/Blood Bank Manual policy BB-15  
 
Purpose:   
To provide guidelines for storage of blood in the Operating Room. 
 
Applies To:   
Surgical Services, Blood Bank 
 
Policy:   
When multiple units are issued to surgery, the units must be stored in an insulated container that can maintain the 
blood between 1-10 degrees C.  (Preferably 2-6 degrees C).  The insulated container must contain ice packs.  Direct 
contact of the packed red blood cells and ice  packs should be avoided.  Dry ice must not be used because of the 
danger of freezing and hemolysis.  Surgical Services shall return any unused blood at the completion of the surgical 
procedure or by 8 hours. 
 
Responsibility:  
Surgical Services Staff, Blood Bank Personnel 
 
 
Procedure: 
1. When multiple units are requested and stored in the blood cooler, the Blood Bank personnel shall affix 
temperature monitoring devices, a thermometer and a temperature log, and place them in an insulated 
container with ice packs. 
2. The container shall be taken to the OR suite where the surgical procedure is in progress. 
3. The temperature indicator on the blood bag shall be checked as acceptable before units are infused.  
4. Blood issued in ice chests is good for 8 hours.  If surgery is longer, return to the Blood Bank for ice packs to 
be replaced. The container/cooler temperature shall be checked hourly and logged by OR circulator on the 
designated form.  
5. When the surgical procedure is completed, the unused blood/blood product shall be returned to the Blood 
Bank in the container with the temperature log. 
6. The OR staff member returning the blood/blood product shall initial the worksheet. 
7. Blood Bank personnel shall check the temperature to ensure acceptability of returned units.  
8. If only a single unit has been requested for transfusion, it must be transfused immediately, or returned to 
blood bank within 30 minutes. (otherwise, blood bank must discard the unit)
