Where are the IT policies located?

FMOLHS Information Technology Policy Summation 
 
All FMOLHS Security policies follow the HHS/OCR HIPAA Security standards. Below is a summation of FMOLHS 
policies as they relate to the HIPAA Security standards. The full policy is available upon request. 
 
Name of the 
FMOLHS Policy 
HIPAA Rule Brief Description of the Policy/HIPAA Rule 
IS.01.000 Security 
Management 
Process 
§ 164.308(a)(1) Implement policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain and 
correct security violations. 
IS.01.001 Risk 
Analysis Policy - § 
164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) 
§ 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A) Conduct an accurate and thorough assessment of the potential 
risks and vulnerabilities to the confidentiality, integrity, and 
availability of electronic protected health information held by the 
covered entity. 
IS.01.002 Risk 
Management Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(B) Implement security measures sufficient to reduce risks and 
vulnerabilities to a reasonable and appropriate level to comply with 
§164.306(a). 
IS.01.003 Sanction 
Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(C) Apply appropriate sanctions against workforce members who fail to 
comply with the security policies and procedures of the covered 
entity. 
IS.01.004 
Information System 
Activity Review 
Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(1)(ii)(D) Implement procedures to regularly review records of information 
system activity, such as audit logs, access reports, and security 
incident tracking reports. 
IS.01.005 Change 
Management 
n/a This policy manages the IT Change Management processes. 
IS.02.000 Assigned 
Security 
§ 164.308(a)(2) Identify the security official who is responsible for the development 
and implementation of the policies and procedures required by this 
subpart [the Security Rule] for the entity.

IS.03.000 Workforce 
Security Policy 
 
§ 164.308(a)(3) Implement policies and procedures to ensure that all members of 
its workforce have appropriate access to electronic protected health 
information, as provided under [the Information Access 
Management standard], and to prevent those workforce members 
who do not have access under [the Information Access 
Management standard] from obtaining access to electronic 
protected health information. 
IS.03.001 
Authorization and 
Supervision Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(3)(ii)(A) Where the Authorization and/or Supervision implementation 
specification is a reasonable and appropriate safeguard for a 
covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement procedures for the authorization and/or supervision of 
workforce members who work with electronic protected health 
information or in locations where it might be accessed. 
IS.03.002 Workforce 
Clearance Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(3)(ii)(B) Covered entities need to address whether all members of the 
workforce with authorized access to EPHI receive appropriate 
clearances. Where the Workforce Clearance Procedure 
implementation specification is a reasonable and appropriate 
safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement procedures to determine that the access of a workforce 
member to electronic protected health information is appropriate. 
IS.03.003 Access 
Termination Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C) Where the Termination Procedures implementation specification is 
a reasonable and appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the 
covered entity must: 
 
Implement procedures for terminating access to electronic 
protected health information when the employment of a workforce 
member ends or as required by determinations made as specified 
in paragraph (a)(3)(ii)(B) [the Workforce Clearance Procedure] of 
this section. 
IS.04.000 
Information Access 
Management Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(4) Implement policies and procedures for authorizing access to 
electronic protected health information that are consistent with the 
applicable requirements of subpart E of this part [the Privacy Rule]. 
IS.04.002 Access 
Authorization Policy  
§ 164.308(a)(4)(ii)(B) Where this implementation standard is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement policies and procedures for granting access to electronic 
protected health information, for example, through access to a 
workstation, transaction, program, process, or other mechanism. 
IS.04.003 Access 
Establishment and 
Modification Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(4)(ii)(C) Where the Access Establishment and Modification implementation 
specification is a reasonable and appropriate safeguard for a 
covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement policies and procedures that, based upon the entity’s 
access authorization policies, establish, document, review, and 
modify a user’s right of access to a workstation, transaction, 
program, or process. 
IS.05.000 Security 
Awareness Training 
Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(5) Implement a security awareness and training program for all 
members of its workforce (including management). 
IS.05.001 Security 
Reminders Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(5)(ii)(A) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must 
implement: 
 
Periodic security updates. 3 
 
IS.05.002 Malicious 
Software Protection 
Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(5)(ii)(B) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must 
implement: 
 
Procedures for guarding against, detecting, and reporting malicious 
software. 
IS.05.003 Log-in 
Monitoring Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(5)(ii)(C) Where the Log-in Monitoring implementation specification is a 
reasonable and appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the 
covered entity must implement: 
 
Procedures for monitoring log-in attempts and reporting 
discrepancies. 
IS.05.004 Password 
Management Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(5)(ii)(D) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must 
implement: 
 
Procedures for creating, changing, and safeguarding passwords. 
IS.06.000 Security 
Incident Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(6) Implement policies and procedures to address security incidents. 
 
Identify and respond to suspected or known security incidents; 
mitigate, to the extent practicable, harmful effects of security 
incidents that are known to the covered entity; and document 
security incidents and their outcomes. 
IS.07.000 
Contingency Plan 
Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(7) Establish (and implement as needed) policies and procedures for 
responding to an emergency or other occurrence (for example, fire, 
vandalism, system failure, and natural disaster) that damages 
systems that contain electronic protected health information. 
IS.07.001 Data 
Backup Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(7)(ii)(A) Establish and implement procedures to create and maintain 
retrievable exact copies of electronic protected health information. 
IS.07.002 Disaster 
Recovery Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(7)(ii)(B) Establish (and implement as needed) procedures to restore any 
loss of data. 
IS.07.003 
Emergency Mode 
Operations Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(7)(ii)(C) Establish (and implement as needed) procedures to enable 
continuation of critical business processes for protection of the 
security of electronic protected health information while operating in 
emergency mode. 
IS.07.004 Testing 
and Revision Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(7)(ii)(D) Where the Testing and Revision Procedures implementation 
specification is a reasonable and appropriate safeguard for the 
covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement procedures for periodic testing and revision of 
contingency plans. 
IS.07.005 
Application and 
Data Criticality 
Analysis Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(7)(ii)(E) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for the covered entity, the covered entity 
must: 
 
Assess the relative criticality of specific applications and data in 
support of other contingency plan components. 
IS.08.000 Evaluation 
and Review of 
Security Measures 
Policy 
§ 164.308(a)(8) Perform a periodic technical and nontechnical evaluation, based 
initially upon the standards implemented under this rule and 
subsequently, in response to environmental or operations changes 
affecting the security of electronic protected health information, that 
establishes the extent to which an entity’s security policies and 
procedures meet the requirements of this subpart [the Security 
Rule]. 
IS.09.000 Business 
Associates Policy 
§ 164.308(b)(1) A covered entity, in accordance with § 164.306 [the Security 
Standards: General Rules], may permit a business associate to 
create, receive, maintain, or transmit electronic protected health 
information on the covered entity’s behalf only if the covered entity 
obtains satisfactory assurances, in accordance with § 164.314(a) 4 
 
[the Organizational Requirements] that the business associate will 
appropriately safeguard the information (Emphasis added). 
IS.10.001 
Contingency 
Operations Policy 
§ 164.310(a)(2)(i) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Establish (and implement as needed) procedures that allow facility 
access in support of restoration of lost data under the disaster 
recovery plan and emergency mode operations plan in the event of 
an emergency. 
IS.10.002 Facility 
Security Policy 
§ 164.310(a)(2)(ii) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement policies and procedures to safeguard the facility and the 
equipment therein from unauthorized physical access, tampering, 
and theft. 
IS.10.003 Access 
Control and 
Validation Policy 
§ 164.310(a)(2)(iii) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement procedures to control and validate a person’s access to 
facilities based on their role or function, including visitor control, and 
control of access to software programs for testing and revision. 
IS.10.004 
Maintenance 
Records Policy 
§ 164.310(a)(2)(iv) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement policies and procedures to document repairs and 
modifications to the physical components of a facility which are 
related to security (for example, hardware, walls, doors and locks). 
IS.11.000 
Workstation Use 
Policy 
§ 164.310(b) A workstation is defined in the rule as an electronic computing 
device, for example, a laptop or desktop computer, or any other 
device that performs similar functions, and electronic media stored 
in its immediate environment. 
 
 Implement policies and procedures that specify the proper 
functions to be performed, the manner in which those functions are 
to be performed, and the physical attributes of the surroundings of 
a specific workstation or class of workstation that can access 
electronic protected health information. 
IS.12.000 
Workstation 
Security Policy 
§ 164.310(c) Implement physical safeguards for all workstations that access 
electronic protected health information, to restrict access to 
authorized users. 
IS.13.001 Media 
Disposal Policy 
§ 164.310(d)(2)(i) Implement policies and procedures to address the final disposition 
of electronic protected health information, and/or the hardware or 
electronic media on which it is stored. 
IS.13.002 Media Re-
Use Policy 
§ 164.310(d)(2)(ii) Implement procedures for removal of electronic protected health 
information from electronic media before the media are made 
available for re-use. 
IS.13.003 Hardware 
and Media 
Accountability 
Policy 
§ 164.310(d)(2)(iii) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Maintain a record of the movements of hardware and electronic 
media and any person responsible therefore. 
IS.13.004 Data 
Backup & Storage 
Policy 
§ 164.310(d)(2)(iv) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Create a retrievable, exact copy of electronic protected health 
information, when needed, before movement of equipment. 
IS.14.001 Unique 
User I.D. Policy 
§ 164.312(a)(2)(i) Assign a unique name and/or number for identifying and tracking 
user identity. 5 
 
IS.14.002 
Emergency Access 
Procedure Policy 
§ 164.312(a)(2)(ii) Establish (and implement as needed) procedures for obtaining 
necessary electronic protected health information during an 
emergency. 
IS.14.003 Automatic 
Log-Off Policy 
 
§ 164.312(a)(2)(iii) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement electronic procedures that terminate an electronic 
session after a predetermined time of inactivity. 
IS.14.004 
Encryption & 
Decryption Policy 
§ 164.312(a)(2)(iv) Where this implementation specification is a reasonable and 
appropriate safeguard for a covered entity, the covered entity must: 
 
Implement a mechanism to encrypt and decrypt electronic 
protected health information. 
IS.15.000 Audit 
Controls Policy 
§ 164.312(b) Implement hardware, software, and/or procedural mechanisms that 
record and examine activity in information systems that contain or 
use electronic protected health information. 
IS.16.000 Data 
Integrity Controls 
Policy 
§ 164.312(c)(1) Integrity is defined in the Security Rule, at § 164.304, as the 
property that data or information have not been altered or 
destroyed in an unauthorized manner. 
 
Implement policies and procedures to protect electronic protected 
health information from improper alteration or destruction. 
IS.17.000 Person or 
Entity 
Authentication 
Policy - § 164.312(d) 
§ 164.312(d) The Person or Entity Authentication standard has no 
implementation specifications.  This standard requires a covered 
entity to: 
 
Implement procedures to verify that a person or entity seeking 
access to electronic protected health information is the one 
claimed. 
IS.18.000 
Transmission 
Security Policy - § 
164.312(e)(1) 
§ 164.312(e)(1) Implement technical security measures to guard against 
unauthorized access to electronic protected health information that 
is being transmitted over an electronic communications network. 
IS.100.001 Internet 
Access and On-Line 
Services Policy 
 
IS.100.002 E-mail 
Usage Policy 
 Define appropriate use of Internet access and e-mail usage to 
include, but not limited to, within the organization, on the company 
network, with company devices and when doing business on behalf 
of the company 
IS.102.000 Mobile 
Device Policy 
 Define appropriate use of a mobile device when, but not limited to, 
conducting company business, accessing PHI or sensitive 
information on a mobile device 
IS.103.000 
Acceptable Use 
Policy 
 Define appropriate use of, but not limited to, resources on or off the 
company network or behalf of the company. 
IS.104.000 Cloud 
Computing Policy 
 Define appropriate use of a cloud resource to include, but not 
limited to, access to cloud resources, sharing/storing of information 
in the cloud and using cloud resources for computational purposes 
IS.105.000 IS 
Exception to 
Policies Policy 
 Define a process for exceptions to IS policies that cannot be met 
due to patient safety or business/operational requirements.
