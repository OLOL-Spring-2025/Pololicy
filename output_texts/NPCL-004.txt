PURPOSE: 
To decrease anxiety, fears, and misconceptions related to upcoming surgery. 
To familiarize children and parents with the hospital environment, procedures, and staff. 
To educate, give information, and offer coping mechanisms. 
 
POLICY: 
The Child Life Department will offer pre-surgery teaching sessions, by appointment, to children who will have 
surgery on an outpatient or inpatient basis.  Children will be educated and prepared for the surgical procedure 
and hospital stay, by a child life specialist. 
1. Appointments will be made by calling the Child Life Department at least 2 to 3 weeks prior to scheduled 
surgery. 
 
2. The pre-surgery teaching session will be facilitated by a child life specialist and will include: 
a. An informal assessment of the child’s level of understanding, developmental level, previous 
experiences with hospitalization, stressors, misconceptions, etc., in order to identify the child’s 
needs. 
b. A tour of the surgical area and a hospital room 
c. An explanation related to the sequence of events, which will occur. 
d. Familiarization with medical equipment. 
e. Education related to sensory expectations, role identification, and coping mechanisms.
