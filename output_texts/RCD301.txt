PURPOSE: To ensure frequent and thorough documentation of patient progress. 
POLICY: Progress notes shall be written to include patient progress in relation to treatment plan/goals. 
PROCEDURE: 
 
1. Each treatment rendered shall be documented on the patient’s medical record.  Treatment documentation shall 
be written at the time of each treatment session.  These notes must include the number of minutes a patient 
was seen in therapy session. 
2. Treatment/Progress notes must be entered by a qualified, licensed therapist or therapist assistant Treatment 
refusals and reason for refusal must also be documented. 
3. Treatment Documentation notes must reflect progress or regression.  If they reflect regression, documentation 
must show evidence of the cause of regression.  If there is a significant change in the patient’s status, a 
reassessment must be completed by a qualified therapist and documentation must be recorded, as such.  
Documentation should include patient’s current status, and any changes in treatment plans should be made 
accordingly. 
4. Treatment refusals and the reason for refusal must be documented. 
5. The plan of care must be reviewed at each treatment session. The plan of care must be updated on a weekly 
basis. Updates to short and long term goals and changes to treatment interventions may be included in these 
updates. 
6. Only hospital approved abbreviations may be used in treatment documentation records and weekly progress 
notes.
