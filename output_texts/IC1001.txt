PURPOSE: 
The purpose of this policy is to maintain an integrated, organization -wide approach to 
identifying and reducing the risks of acquiring and transmitting infections among patients, staff 
and others. 
 
POLICY 
I. The Quality and Safety Committee oversees the Infection Prevention and Control 
Program within the medical center. 
II. The Committee, or its designee, has the authority to institute any surveillance, 
prevention and control measures, or studies when there is reason to believe that any 
patient or personnel may be in danger. 
III. The Infection Control Staff conducts surveillance, trends analysis and statistical 
computation utilizing the Centers for Disease Control (CDC) National Healthcare 
Safety Network (NHSN) criteria for determining presence and classification of 
hospital acquired infections. 
IV. The Quality and Safety  Committee has approved the use of Centers for Disease 
Control and Prevention (CDC) and the Hospital Infection Control Practices 
Advisory Committee (HICPAC) revised "2007 Guideline for Isolation Precautions: 
Preventing Transmission of Infectious Agents in Healthcare Settings.” 
The hospital will reserve the right to isolate patients according to the CDC 
Guidelines.  The infection control practitioner or the Medical Director of Infection 
Prevention and Control will have the ultimate authority to make decisions regarding 
isolation precautions when conflicts arise. 
V. All healthcare workers will be familiar with and utilize Standard Precautions in the 
care of all patients in order to minimize contact with blood and/or body fluids. 
VI. Communicable diseases as listed by Louisiana Sanitary Code  Part II: Chapter 1  
§103-§105 will be reported. 
VII. Environmental cultures must receive prior approval by the Medical Director of 
Infection Prevention and Control. 
VIII. Infection Prevention and Control provides input into the content and scope of the 
Employee Health Program.
