PURPOSE: 
To ensure the safety of patients, visitors, and team members in the event of a fire or to the conditions 
that indicate the presence of a fire. To prepare staff to appropriately respond to the danger fires present 
by addressing aspects of fire prevention, preparedness, control, and education.  To delineate authority, 
responsibilities, and procedures to follow in the event of a fire. 
 
POLICY: 
In the event of a fire, all staff will follow Our Lady of the Lake (OLOL) Regional Medical Center’s 
written Code RED Safety and Response Plan to guide and prepare all in the proper response to fires.  
 
Contractors, students, licensed independent practitioners (LIP), and vendors are to follow the instruction 
of unit manager or designee. 
 
Each OLOL department will have their own Department Specific Code RED Safety and Response Plan. 
 
Each location’s Code RED Safety and Response Plan will be used in conjunction with the Emergency 
Operations Plan (ECEP001) and the OLOL Code RED Safety and Response Plan (ECEP007) to guide 
the preparation and response in the event of a fire.

Specific roles of staff related to Code RED drills or Code RED response 
 
• CNAs or designee will clear the hallway of any carts or equipment by moving them to the back 
corner by the staff lockers 
• CSRs or designee will close all doors 
• Charge Nurse or designee will confirm automatic fire doors closed 
• Nurses working in PACU will move patients and visitors to safety explaining, “The hospital is 
conducting an emergency exercise to ensure your safety” 
• Nurse caring for the patient, or designee, will assist those with Limited English Proficiency 
(LEP) and those with sensory or mobility disabilities 
• Nurse Manager or designee will ensure all employees are accounted for 
• Supervisor or designee will ensure all patients are accounted for 
• Manager or designee will ensure entire room is empty.  
 
The location of medical gas shut off valves and who has authority to shut them off 
The medical gas shut off valves are behind the CSR desk.  The manager or designee will notify either 
Plant Services or Respiratory to turn off the medical gas. 
 
Location and proper use of equipment for transporting patients between Fire/Smoke Compartments 
(horizontal and vertical evacuations) 
• For horizontal evacuations, we will use the stretcher or bed the patient is on. 
• For vertical evacuations, we will use equipment such as stretchers and sheets. Additional sheets 
are located on the linen cart in the Supply hallway. 
• We anticipate to be partially ambulatory. If assistance is needed, the Manager or designee will 
seek help from the OR. 
 
Location and proper use of fire-fighting equipment and manual pull stations. 
• Fire extinguisher is located directly opposite Bay 8. 
• Additional fire extinguisher is located directly outside the exit door by Bay 6. 
• Manual pull station is on the back hallway – near ECHO lab, across from the fire hose 
connection. 
• Additional manual pull station is next to the stairwell to the right of SRNA work room behind 
Elevator E.
