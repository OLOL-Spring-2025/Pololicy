PURPOSE: 
To provide guidelines outlining those procedures not allowed in the Emergency Department.  
POLICY: 
1. The following procedures are NOT allowed: 
a. Removal of fecal impaction/enemas other than Fleets and those to correct critical electrolyte 
abnormalities. 
b. Outpatient IV therapy and/or drug administration.  This includes fluid hydration, antibiotic 
therapy, chemotherapy, and administration of blood or blood products. 
c. Chemotherapy 
d. Peritoneal Dialysis 
e. Sterile procedures requiring more than 30 minutes to complete. 
f. The following orthopedic procedures: 
• Jaw wires 
• Flexor tendon repair 
g. Regarding endoscopy procedures: the only exception being an emergency situation where the 
patient is unstable and/or actively bleeding. 
h. General Anesthesia 
i. Facial fracture surgery including elevation of fractured zygoma's 
j. Burn Treatment (other than minor burns) 
k. Skin Grafts 
l. Lesion excision requiring flap or skin graft reconstruction 
m. Cosmetic surgery 
n. Circumcision 
o. Open scrotal incision 
p. Open suprapubic cystostomy 
q. Inpatient care such as laceration repair, lesion removal
