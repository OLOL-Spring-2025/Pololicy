PURPOSE:  
To ensure a comfortable transition for the patient to the hospital environment and to facilitate 
appropriate placement of patients. 
 
POLICY:  
A patient will be admitted to the Nursing Unit or specialty areas in which is ordered by the admitting 
physician.  Room assignments are made through the electronic medical record.  
 
PROCEDURES: 
1. Access Services will attach an identification band to the wrist of each patient as a part of the regular 
admitting procedure.  The patient will be escorted to the unit by a patient representative who will 
notify the nursing staff of the patient’s arrival.  
2. Admitting orders will either be electronically entered into EMR or escorted to the unit by the patient 
representative along with the patient on admit.   
3. An admitting nurse should verify the patient with the ID band and patient’s chart. 
4. Patient is oriented to the room’s safety, environment and communication features.  
5. The initial admission assessment is completed by a Registered Nurse within the time frame specified 
in the policy Patient Assessment/Reassessment OrgClin/031. 
6. The plan of care will be formulated based upon the assessment and discharge needs should be 
identified at time of admission. 
7. Patient information and admission profile is entered into the computer system, including home 
medications. 
8. Nurses' admitting/assessment documentation shall be completed by admitting nurse. 
9. The admitting physician will be notified of the patient’s arrival to the unit.
